# Bloomberg Terminal Narrative — W20

## The 4 AM Read

The coffee hasn't even finished brewing, but the HORMUZ headline on the terminal is enough to spike the cortisol. 4:17 AM. My phone is a graveyard of alerts. I pull up the Sarasti face grid—the "emotional" heartbeat of the portfolio. In this game, the numbers are often too slow. I need to see the *soul* of the market before the London open.

My eyes go straight to **RB=F** (RBOB Gasoline). It’s screaming. The face is pure, unadulterated shock. High Alarm (0.72) and peak Distress (0.72). It’s not angry—there’s no Aggression (-0.61)—it’s just terrified of the supply chain snapping. The low Vitality (-0.15) tells me the liquidity is thin, gasping for air as the orders pile up. It’s the most honest face on the grid right now.

Next to it, **BRENT** is a different kind of nightmare. It’s hyper-vivid, almost glowing with Vitality (0.97). The price action is moving too fast for the eyes to follow, but the face is slumped in deep Distress (0.56) and a total lack of Aggression (-0.92). It looks like a victim of its own success—the price is mooning, but the market is miserable. It’s a "forced move" face. You see this when the shorts are getting liquidated and no one is happy about it.

I scan down to **_VIX**. It’s looking predictably haggard. High Distress (0.57) and complete exhaustion (Vitality -0.87). It looks like it hasn't slept in a week. When the VIX looks this fatigued, it usually means the panic is starting to institutionalize. It’s not a spike anymore; it’s a regime.

**WTI** is holding a weirdly aggressive pose compared to Brent (Aggression 0.38 vs -0.92). It looks like it’s trying to fight back, maybe betting that the US SPR (Strategic Petroleum Reserve) will blunt the blow. It’s more "youthful" and "active" than Brent right now. I’d buy WTI and sell Brent on this spread—Brent looks like it’s about to have a nervous breakdown, while WTI still has some fight left in it.

**SPY** and **XLE** are twins of misery. Both are showing that middle-of-the-road Distress (0.37) and low Aggression (-0.74). They look like they’re waiting for the other shoe to drop. No panic yet, just a grim realization that the bull market just took a mine to the hull.

The surprise is **ALI=F** (Aluminum). It’s looking actually... aggressive? Aggression at 0.73 and high Vitality. While the rest of the world is mourning, Aluminum is smelling opportunity—or maybe just a massive supply-side squeeze from the same shipping lanes. It looks like a predator. I’m looking for long entries there.

Finally, **GC=F** (Gold). It’s the most "mature" face on the board (Maturity 0.42). It’s calm, distressed (0.29), but totally non-aggressive (-0.84). It’s the face of the adult in the room. It’s the safe haven, and it looks like it knows it. It’s not panicking because it’s the thing people buy when they panic.

I’m hitting the "Sell" button on Brent—that face is overextended. I'm going Long on Aluminum and Gold. The market is scared, but only a few instruments are actually "fighting."

## Trading Signals from Faces Alone

1.  **SHORT BRENT:** The face shows 0.97 vitality but -0.92 aggression. This is a "blow-off top" expression. It’s moving on pure momentum and panic, but there’s no "conviction" (aggression) behind the move. It’s ripe for a mean-reversion as soon as the initial shock fades.
2.  **LONG ALI=F:** Aluminum has the highest combination of Aggression and Vitality. It looks like it’s leading a new trend, not just reacting to one. It’s "hunting."
3.  **LONG GC=F:** Gold’s high Maturity and low Aggression in the face of crisis is a classic "Buy and Hold" signal. It’s the only one that doesn't look like it’s about to cry or scream.
4.  **LONG WTI / SHORT BRENT:** The "Aggression Gap" between WTI (0.38) and Brent (-0.92) is massive. WTI looks fundamentally stronger in its "emotional" profile, likely due to distance from the physical strait. Brent is the one having the panic attack.
