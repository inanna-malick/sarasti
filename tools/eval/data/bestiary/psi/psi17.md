## Description

Psi17 is the left-sided member of the third asymmetric conjugate pair (psi17/psi18). The conjugate pair architecture maps neatly across the face:

| Pair | Region |
|------|--------|
| psi11/psi12 | Mouth corners |
| psi14/psi15 | Brows / upper face |
| psi17/psi18 | Mid-face / cheeks |

At positive values, the left side of the mid-face tightens — there is a subtle left cheek compression or hollowing, and the left nasolabial area adjusts. The right side remains comparatively neutral. The effect is a barely-visible left-sided grimace or tension.

At negative values, the left mid-face relaxes: the cheek fills slightly and the nasolabial area softens.

## Conjugate Pair: psi17 + psi18

At matched weights, psi17 and psi18 combine into bilateral mid-face expression. This would produce a symmetric cheek tension or compression — potentially useful for adding a "held" quality to the mid-face that complements primary expression drivers. The bilateral pair would be a different flavor of mid-face expression than psi3 (which is a primary symmetric component driving disgust/curiosity with much larger amplitude).

## Symmetry

Asymmetric (score ~0.35). Left-lateralized by design. Part of the conjugate pair architecture.
