## psi3 -- Assessment / Disgust Axis

At +3.0, the face is alive with curiosity. The brows lift and separate, the eyes widen with active interest, and the mouth parts slightly in an attentive, questioning expression. The mid-face opens up, as if making space for incoming information. The overall read is someone examining a specimen under glass, or leaning in to catch every word of an important story. At +1.5, this is a subtle engaged alertness -- the face of someone who is paying close attention.

At -3.0, the face contracts into distaste. The nose wrinkles, the upper lip draws upward in a subtle sneer, and the brows knit together over narrowed eyes. The expression reads as disgust, revulsion, or disdain -- someone who has just encountered something offensive. At -1.5, this is mild disapproval or the faint suggestion of "that smells bad."

The transition through zero is smooth and well-behaved. The neutral face sits at rest, and psi3 sweeps cleanly from contracted disdain through rest into open curiosity without any jumps or discontinuities.

The primary affected region is the mid-face: the glabella (area between the brows), the nasion (bridge of the nose), the upper lip, and the periorbital muscles. The lower face is moderately engaged (mouth parting at positive extremes) but the center of gravity is clearly in the middle third of the face.

The positive pole reads as: a scientist peering at unexpected data. An analyst who just noticed an anomaly. "Tell me more."
The negative pole reads as: someone discovering spoiled food. A critic dismissing substandard work. "Ugh."

**Symmetry:** 0.95 -- Excellent bilateral symmetry. The left34 and right34 views at +2.0 confirm clean, mirrored expression on both sides.

**Artifacts:** One of the cleanest components in the entire expression space. No mesh distortion, bunching, or thinning observed at any tested value. Geometry is robust through the full -3.0 to +3.0 range and likely safe well beyond.

**Safe range:** [-4.0, 4.0]

**Currently used in:**
- fatigue_wired recipe at weight 1.5 (open curiosity -- assessing, scanning)
- fatigue_exhausted recipe at weight -0.6 (disgust -- checked out, "whatever")

**Potential uses:** This is the premier "assessment" component. Its positive pole is ideal for any state that requires vigilant, searching attention -- the wired pole of fatigue, suspicious scrutiny, analytical engagement. Its negative pole could serve exhaustion or defeat states where the face collapses into a "can't be bothered" disdain. The mid-face focus makes it complementary to lower-face components (psi0, psi9) and upper-face components (psi8, psi6) without spatial conflict.
