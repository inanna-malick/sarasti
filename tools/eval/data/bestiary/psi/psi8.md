## psi8 -- Shock / Flatness (The Startle Reflex)

At +3.0, the face is in full shock. The eyes widen dramatically -- more than any other component in psi0-psi9 produces at comparable weights. The brows shoot upward, the forehead creases, and the periorbital muscles snap taut. The mouth may part slightly but the center of gravity is overwhelmingly in the upper face: this is a staring, frozen, "deer in headlights" expression. At +1.5, the effect is a heightened alertness -- eyes wider than normal, brows slightly raised, the face of someone who just heard an unexpected noise.

At -3.0, the face goes flat. The eyes narrow to a default width or slightly below, the brows settle into an unremarkable position, and all alertness drains away. The expression reads as blank, bored, emotionally absent. This is not the active disengagement of psi4-negative (which has a "checked out" quality) but a more fundamental flatness -- as if the face has simply stopped registering stimuli. At -1.5, this is a subtle dulling.

The transition through zero is smooth. The neutral face has a default level of alertness, and psi8 dials this dramatically up (shock) or gradually down (flatness). The positive direction is much more visually dramatic than the negative -- going from 0 to +3.0 produces a much larger visual change than going from 0 to -3.0.

The FLAME label "nose wrinkler" is spectacularly wrong for this component. The nose is barely affected. The primary action is in the upper face: the supraorbital region (above the eyes), the eyelids, the forehead, and the brows. This is the startle component, the alarm bell, the widened-eye shock response. If psi7 is the emotional core (contentment vs disappointment), psi8 is the arousal axis (hyper-alert vs flat).

The positive pole reads as: seeing your portfolio drop 40% in a single candle. A deer in headlights. The moment a soldier hears an explosion. The face of someone who CANNOT LOOK AWAY.
The negative pole reads as: the face of someone watching static on a television. Emotional white noise. Nothing is happening and nothing will happen.

**Symmetry:** 0.94 -- Good bilateral symmetry. The shock response widens both eyes equally. Very slight asymmetry in the brow region at the +2.0 3/4 views, but well within acceptable range for bilateral expression use.

**Artifacts:** At +3.0 the eye-widening is dramatic but geometry holds. The upper eyelid lifts aggressively, and past +4.0 there is risk of the eyelid geometry stretching in unnatural ways (the inverse of psi7's eyelid clipping problem). Negative values are very safe -- the flattening effect involves minimal geometric displacement.

**Safe range:** [-4.0, 4.0]

**Currently used in:**
- alarm_alarmed recipe at weight 2.0 (PRIMARY -- whole-face startle) -- this is the single heaviest weight in the alarm-alarmed recipe
- fatigue_wired recipe at weight 0.6 (slightly shocked -- alert edge)

**Potential uses:** Psi8 is already the backbone of the alarm-alarmed state and does not need additional assignments. Its negative pole could theoretically contribute to depression or exhaustion states, but psi7-negative already serves that role with more emotional richness. The main value of psi8 is its specificity: it is the best tool in the expression space for communicating acute surprise and alarm, and it should be reserved for that purpose.
