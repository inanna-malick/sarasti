# psi16 — Chin Projection / Lower Face Firmness

## Visual Summary

| Pole | Read |
|------|------|
| -3.0 | Subtle chin retraction, softened mentalis, marginally receding lower face |
| 0.0  | Neutral baseline |
| +3.0 | Subtle chin forward projection, tightened mentalis, firmed lower face |

## Description

Psi16 is a symmetric modulator of the lower face, centered on the chin and mentalis region. Unlike the primary lower-face components — psi0 (jaw drop / frown-smile) and psi4 (lip pucker / engagement) — psi16 operates on the structural character of the chin itself rather than on the mouth or lips.

At positive values, the chin projects subtly forward and the mentalis tightens. There is a barely-perceptible firming of the lower face that reads, in aggregate, as quiet determination or stubbornness — the jaw sets, the chin juts. The lips are not significantly affected; this is below the mouth.

At negative values, the chin recedes marginally and the mentalis softens. The lower face takes on a slightly weaker or more passive quality. Again, the effect is subtle.

## Symmetry

Bilaterally symmetric. Clean deformation in both 3/4 views. Safe for solo use without conjugate pairing.

## Practical Notes

- **Extremely subtle** at the tested range. Among the least visually impactful components in the psi10-psi19 band alongside psi13.
- Not currently used in any expression recipe.
- Could potentially contribute to the dominance shape axis — its chin projection quality overlaps conceptually with the "chad" pole of dominance — but it operates in expression space (psi) rather than shape space (beta), meaning it would be animated per-frame rather than held static.
- An expression-space chin jut could be interesting for the "wired" fatigue pole, adding a "leaning in" quality to complement the existing pose pitch offset.
- No mesh artifacts at any tested weight.

## Confidence

Low. Subtle effects at tested weights make precise characterization difficult. The chin/mentalis attribution is a best-effort observation.
