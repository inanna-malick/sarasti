# psi15 — Right Brow / Right Upper Face (Conjugate: psi14)

## Description

Psi15 is the right-handed mirror of psi14 — the second member of the upper-face conjugate pair. It lateralizes brow and periorbital movement to the right side of the face.

At positive values, the right brow lifts subtly and the right periorbital region widens. The left side remains comparatively neutral. At negative values, the right upper face narrows marginally.

This mirrors psi14 exactly, but on the opposite side. See psi14.md for the full conjugate pair analysis.

## Conjugate Pair: psi14 + psi15

At matched weights, psi14 and psi15 combine into bilateral upper-face expression. This pair provides finer-grained brow control than psi2 (a symmetric primary component with larger amplitude and more whole-face involvement). See psi14.md for details.

## Symmetry

Asymmetric (score ~0.4). Right-lateralized by design. Mirrors psi14 across the sagittal plane.

## Practical Notes

- Not currently used in any expression recipe.
- Identical usage guidelines to psi14, but lateralized right.
- **Solo use** = right-sided brow lift / quizzical look.
- **Paired with psi14** = bilateral brow expression.
- No mesh artifacts at tested weights.

## Confidence

Low-medium. Structural confidence from the documented conjugate pair architecture. Visual characterization is limited by the subtlety of mid-frequency components at the tested weight range.
