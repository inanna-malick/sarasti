# psi13 — Upper Face Aperture / Periorbital Modulator

## Visual Summary

| Pole | Read |
|------|------|
| -3.0 | Very subtle upper face narrowing, mild periorbital contraction |
| 0.0  | Neutral baseline |
| +3.0 | Very subtle upper face opening, mild periorbital widening, forehead smoothing |

## Description

Psi13 operates in the upper face — the periorbital region and forehead — but at a much lower amplitude than the primary upper-face components (psi2, psi7). Where psi2 drives dramatic mouth opening with brow involvement and psi7 modulates the eyelids in a happy/disappointed axis, psi13 provides a subtle adjustment to the aperture of the eye region and the tension state of the forehead.

At positive values, there is a barely-perceptible opening of the eye area. The forehead smooths and the periorbital region widens marginally. The impression — if one can call something this subtle an impression — is of a gentle alertness or receptivity in the upper face. It is the difference between a face at rest and a face that has just become slightly more attentive.

At negative values, the effect reverses: a very mild closing-down of the upper face, a tightening of the forehead, a narrowing of the eye region. Not a squint, not a frown — just a fractional reduction in upper-face openness.

## Symmetry

Bilaterally symmetric. The 3/4 left and right views at +2.0 show consistent deformation. Safe for solo use.

## Practical Notes

- **Extremely subtle** at the tested range. This is one of the least visually impactful components in the psi10-psi19 band.
- No mesh artifacts at any weight tested. Could safely be driven to higher weights if needed.
- Not currently used in any expression recipe.
- Potential use: adding a very subtle layer of upper-face openness or closure to complement primary expression drivers. Could contribute to differentiating the "wired" (open, alert) vs "exhausted" (closed, guarded) fatigue axis in the upper face.
- Would need higher weights (perhaps 3-5x normal) to be visually meaningful in the context of a face already deformed by primary components.

## Confidence

Low. The deformations are at the threshold of reliable visual characterization from static renders. Interactive exploration at higher weights would significantly improve confidence.
