## Description

Psi12 is the right-handed mirror of psi11 — the second member of the first documented asymmetric conjugate pair. Everything said about psi11 applies here in mirror: the deformation is lateralized to the right side of the face.

At positive values, the right mouth corner lifts upward and outward. Right-side cheek bunching and nasolabial deepening accompany the lift. The left side remains largely neutral. The 3/4 right view shows the effect more prominently than the left view.

At negative values, the right mouth corner depresses, creating a right-sided downturn.

## Conjugate Pair: psi11 + psi12

The practical purpose of psi12 is to pair with psi11. At equal weights, the two halves combine into a bilateral smile with a character distinct from psi9 — more mouth-corner focused, more wry and knowing. This is the **primary smile driver** in the current euphoric expression recipe.

See psi11.md for full conjugate pair analysis.

## Symmetry

Strongly asymmetric (score ~0.3). Mirrors psi11 across the sagittal plane. This is by design.

## Practical Notes

- Identical usage guidelines to psi11, but lateralized right.
- **Solo use** = right-sided smirk.
- **Paired with psi11** = bilateral smile.

- No mesh artifacts at tested weights.

Medium. Well-characterized through both explorer testing and render analysis. Conjugate pair relationship is the most verified aspect of the mid-frequency components.
