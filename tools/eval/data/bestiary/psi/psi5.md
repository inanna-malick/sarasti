## psi5 -- Frown / Slack Indifference

At +3.0, the face settles into a distinct frown. The mouth corners pull downward, the brows draw together and slightly down, and the upper lip lifts subtly in what might be called a sneer or grimace. The overall read is active displeasure -- someone who is frustrated, annoyed, or clenching against something unpleasant. This is not sadness (that is more psi7-negative territory) but rather irritation, a tight and effortful negative expression. At +1.5, this is a subtle tightening of the face -- the faint clenching before someone says something they will regret.

At -3.0, the face goes flat and slack. Whatever muscular engagement the frown required is released, and what remains is pure indifference. The mouth is neutral-to-slightly-open, the brows relaxed, the eyes empty of purpose. The expression reads as someone who could not possibly care less. At -1.5, this is the earliest stage of disengagement -- attention beginning to drain away.

The transition through zero is smooth and gradual. Psi5 is one of the more subtle components in the first ten -- its effect is less about dramatic shape change and more about muscular tone and the quality of facial tension. This subtlety can make it hard to read in isolation, but in recipes it contributes a noticeable tonal layer.

Despite the FLAME label "upper lip raiser" (suggesting the levator labii muscle), the actual visual effect is holistic. The entire mid-face participates: brows engage, the nose bridge tenses slightly, mouth corners drop, and there is a general sense of facial contraction at the positive pole. The "upper lip raiser" label may reflect the PCA origin from muscle-action-unit data, but what emerges perceptually is a frown gesture, not an isolated lip movement.

The positive pole reads as: someone who just received an unfair parking ticket. A teacher whose students are not paying attention. Grinding through unpleasant work.
The negative pole reads as: the face of someone scrolling past content without registering it. Emotional flatline. "Whatever."

**Symmetry:** 0.95 -- Good bilateral symmetry. The frown and slack states are both symmetric expressions.

**Artifacts:** Clean and well-behaved. Because psi5 works through subtle tonal changes rather than dramatic deformation, mesh artifacts are essentially absent at all tested values.

**Safe range:** [-4.0, 4.0]

**Currently used in:**
- fatigue_wired recipe at weight 1.2 (frown -- tight, clenched)
- fatigue_exhausted recipe at weight -0.8 (uninterested -- slack face)

**Potential uses:** Psi5 is a tonal seasoning rather than a structural pillar. It adds the quality of effort and frustration (positive) or vacancy (negative) to any recipe. It could contribute to tension or alarm states where the face needs to convey effortful resistance rather than passive reception. Its mid-face focus puts it in the same spatial channel as psi3, so they compete slightly -- but their perceptual characters are distinct enough (curiosity vs. displeasure) to layer at moderate weights.
