# psi10 — Mid-Face Tension Modulator

## Visual Summary

| Pole | Read |
|------|------|
| -3.0 | Slight facial softening, relaxed mid-face, nasolabial folds eased |
| 0.0  | Neutral baseline |
| +3.0 | Subtle tightening of the nasolabial corridor, contained quality |

## Description

Psi10 is the first component beyond the primary expression battery (psi0-psi9), and its effects are correspondingly subtle. Where the first ten components each command a recognizable holistic expression shift, psi10 operates as a modulator — it adjusts the *tone* of the mid-face rather than driving an expression outright.

In positive values, there is a barely-perceptible tightening across the nasolabial region. The cheeks flatten slightly and the face takes on a quality of muscular containment, as though the subject is holding back a reaction — bracing. The effect is not dramatic enough to register as any particular emotion at the tested weights, but in combination with other active components it would add a "clenched" undercurrent.

In negative values, the mid-face softens. The nasolabial folds relax, and there is a very mild broadening quality. The overall impression is of release or slackness — not the dramatic drooping of psi7's negative pole, but a gentler easing.

## Symmetry

Bilaterally symmetric. The 3/4 left and right views at +2.0 show consistent deformation on both sides. No left-right bias detected. Safe for solo use without conjugate pairing.

## Practical Notes

- **Very subtle** at the ranges tested (-3 to +3). The visual delta from neutral requires careful A/B comparison to discern.
- No mesh artifacts observed at any tested value. Being a lower-variance PCA component, it inherently stays within safe deformation territory at normal weights.
- Most useful as a **layering component** — adding low-weight psi10 on top of a primary expression recipe to introduce a quality of held tension or released slackness.
- Not currently used in any expression recipe. Could contribute to the wired/exhausted fatigue axis as a subtle mid-face tone modifier.

## Confidence

Low. The deformations at these weights are subtle enough that confident characterization requires higher-weight renders or interactive exploration. The descriptions above represent best-effort observation from the available renders.
