## psi9 -- Smile / Frown (The Symmetric Smile)

At +3.0, the face breaks into a full, warm, genuine smile. The mouth widens, the corners pull upward, the cheeks lift and round, and the nasolabial folds deepen into clear parenthetical curves framing the mouth. This is the most natural, balanced smile available in the first ten expression components -- more symmetric than psi1 (which produces a lopsided smirk), more purely smile-like than psi0 (which combines smile with jaw drop), and more mouth-focused than psi7 (which centers on the eyes). At +1.5, this is a pleasant, moderate smile -- the kind that greets a friend.

At -3.0, the face settles into a symmetric frown. The mouth corners pull down, the cheeks flatten, and the overall expression reads as deflated, unhappy, or saddened. This is not the active displeasure of psi5-positive (which has a clenched, frustrated quality) but a more passive sadness -- things are bad and energy is draining. At -1.5, this is a mild downturn, a face beginning to register bad news.

The transition through zero is smooth and emotionally readable at every point. The neutral face has a relaxed, neither-smiling-nor-frowning baseline, and psi9 sweeps cleanly from frown through neutral into smile. This linearity and emotional clarity make psi9 one of the most useful components in the entire expression space.

Despite the FLAME label "cheek puffer," the cheek-puffing effect is secondary to the smile/frown character. There is a slight rounding of the cheeks at extreme positive values (the "puff" the label references), but what you perceive is a smile, not inflated cheeks. The primary affected region is the lower face: mouth, cheeks, nasolabial folds. The upper face is moderately engaged (slight cheek lift affects under-eye area), but the mouth is the star.

The positive pole reads as: hearing that the crisis has been averted. A relief rally. The face of someone whose long position just paid off.
The negative pole reads as: watching profits evaporate. A trend reversal. The slow realization that the thesis was wrong.

**Symmetry:** 0.94 -- Good bilateral symmetry, though very slightly less perfect than psi0 or psi4. The left34 and right34 views at +2.0 show a consistent, balanced smile on both sides. Any asymmetry is below the threshold of readability.

**Artifacts:** Clean through the full tested range. At +3.0 the smile is broad but geometry is intact -- no mesh thinning, inversion, or bunching. At -3.0 the frown is clear but equally clean. The slight cheek rounding at extreme positive values reads naturally and does not produce distortion.

**Safe range:** [-4.0, 4.0]

**Currently used in:**
- alarm_euphoric recipe at weight 2.5 (smile -- reinforces smile character) -- this is the heaviest weight in the euphoric recipe

**Potential uses:** Psi9 is the workhorse smile component. Its negative pole (frown) is currently unused in recipes and could contribute to grief, sadness, or pessimism states. At moderate negative weights it could complement psi7-negative (disappointment) to create a face that is both eye-sad and mouth-sad. The positive pole is already well-utilized in the euphoric recipe. Given its clean behavior and emotional clarity, psi9 could safely be used at higher weights (up to 3.0) if more dramatic euphoria is needed, though the current 2.5 is already near the practical maximum for a single component.
