## psi2 -- Open Intensity / Aggressive Mouth

At +3.0, the face is in a full-throated yell. The mouth opens wide, but unlike psi0's friendly gape, this opening carries tension and aggression. The brows draw down slightly, the mid-face muscles engage, and the nasolabial folds cut deep furrows. The overall read is someone shouting a command, snarling at a threat, or making a fierce demand. At +1.5, this is a milder intensity -- mouth open in surprise or forceful speech.

At -3.0, the face clamps shut. Lips press together firmly with an underbite quality, as if the jaw is thrust slightly forward while the mouth refuses to open. The expression reads as resolute, stubborn, or stonewalling. At -1.5, this is a subtle tightening -- someone clenching their jaw before responding.

The transition through zero is smooth but notably nonlinear. From 0 to +1.5 the change is moderate (mouth opens, face adds tension). From +1.5 to +3.0 there is a dramatic intensification -- the expression snaps from "surprised" to "screaming." This makes psi2 useful as an intensity dial where moderate weights produce subtle alertness but high weights produce dramatic alarm.

Despite the FLAME label "brow raise," the primary action is in the mouth and mid-face. The brows do engage at extreme values, but the mouth opening with aggressive tension is the dominant visual feature. This is an open-mouth-anger component more than a brow component.

The positive pole reads as: a drill sergeant issuing orders. A trader screaming on a floor. Outrage at a betrayal.
The negative pole reads as: a poker face. "No comment." Refusing to dignify something with a response.

**Symmetry:** 0.90 -- Good bilateral symmetry. Both the left34 and right34 views show consistent, symmetric mouth opening and mid-face tension.

**Artifacts:** Clean through the tested range. At +3.0 the mouth opening is dramatic but the mesh holds. The main concern is the nonlinear intensification past +2.0, which can make expressions jump from subtle to extreme faster than expected.

**Safe range:** [-4.0, 4.0]

**Currently used in:**
- alarm_alarmed recipe at weight 1.0 (open mouth intensity)

**Potential uses:** Primary intensity amplifier for alarm states. Could also serve aggression or dominance expression overlays if the project ever adds those. The negative pole (clamped jaw) could contribute to stoic or resolute states. Useful wherever the expression needs to convey forceful engagement rather than passive reception.
