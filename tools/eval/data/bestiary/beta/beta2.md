# Beta2 — Midface Width and Nasal Breadth

**Axis:** narrow/pinched midface (-) ... wide/broad midface (+)

## What It Does

Beta2 is the third principal component and it homes in on the midface — the horizontal band from cheekbone to cheekbone through the nose. Where beta0 scales everything and beta1 stretches vertically, beta2 is the first component with a clear regional bias.

## Positive Pole (+1.5 to +3.0): The Wide Midface

Pushing positive expands the face laterally through its widest point. The cheekbones push outward and become more prominent. The nose widens — both the bridge and the alar base spread, creating a broader, flatter nasal structure. The orbital area appears slightly wider-set. The overall effect is a face that reads as broad and robust through the middle, even if the forehead and jaw remain relatively unaffected.

At +1.5 the widening is subtle but clear — a person with wide cheekbones and a broad nose. At +3.0 it becomes the dominant structural feature: a conspicuously wide midface with a flat, spread nose.

The 3/4 views at +2.0 confirm that the cheekbone projection increases — these are not just soft tissue changes. The zygomatic arch pushes further laterally, creating a more pronounced cheekbone contour in profile.

## Negative Pole (-1.5 to -3.0): The Narrow Midface

Pulling negative compresses the midface toward the sagittal plane. The cheekbones flatten, the nose narrows (especially at the bridge), and the face becomes more pinched through the middle. At -1.5 it reads as a face with fine, narrow features. At -3.0 the midface is visibly compressed — the nose is thin, the cheekbones are absent, and the face looks pinched and somewhat gaunt through the middle.

## Symmetry

High symmetry (0.96). The widening/narrowing applies evenly to both sides, as confirmed by the 3/4 views.

## Safety

Clean through the full range. No mesh artifacts at the extremes. The nose geometry stays coherent even at +3.0, though the bridge becomes quite flat and wide. At -3.0 the narrowing creates some visual oddity (the face looks unusually pinched) but the mesh holds.

## Current Usage

Used in the **dominance** recipe with weight 2.5. Positive dominance adds midface width — wide cheekbones read as structurally dominant in face perception research. This is a well-supported application: zygomatic width is one of the strongest cross-cultural predictors of perceived dominance in real faces.

## Interactions

- With **beta0**: both add facial width, but beta0 does it globally while beta2 concentrates on the midface. Using both at +2.5 in the dominance recipe creates a face that is broadly massive AND specifically wide through the cheekbones — a double dose that is effective but should be monitored for over-stacking at extreme dominance values.
- With **beta4**: both affect the nose. Beta2 widens the nasal bridge; beta4 has its own nose-related effects. The interaction is generally additive and not problematic.

## Naturalist Notes

The midface width axis captures real anthropometric variation. Populations with wider faces (higher bizygomatic breadth) naturally cluster at positive beta2. The connection between wide midface and perceived dominance/physical strength is well-documented in evolutionary psychology — fWHR (facial width-to-height ratio) is a widely studied metric, and beta2 is the FLAME model's version of the width component of that ratio.
