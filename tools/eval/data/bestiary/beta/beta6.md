# Beta6 — Cranial Vault Height

**Axis:** flat/low cranial vault (-) ... tall/domed cranial vault (+)

## What It Does

Beta6 raises or lowers the roof. It targets the cranial vault — the dome of the skull above the brow line — without substantially altering the facial features themselves. The nose, mouth, jaw, and eyes remain largely unaffected while the top of the head rises or falls.

## Positive Pole (+1.5 to +3.0): The Domed Cranium

Pushing positive lifts the cranial vault, making the forehead taller and the overall head silhouette higher from brow to crown. The forehead gains a more rounded, domed quality. The effective hairline (if one imagined hair) would be higher. At +1.5 the effect is subtle — a slightly taller head. At +3.0 the head is visibly taller and more domed, though the change is concentrated above the face rather than in it.

## Negative Pole (-1.5 to -3.0): The Flat Top

Pulling negative compresses the vault, creating a lower, flatter cranial silhouette. The head appears squatter. The forehead shortens. At extremes, the head looks unusually compact above the brow line — all face, less skull.

## Subtlety Level

This is one of the subtler components. Because it primarily affects the top of the head rather than the feature-rich facial area (eyes, nose, mouth, jaw), the visual impact in frontal photographs is modest compared to beta0-beta4. The change is most legible in the overall head silhouette and in profile views. In a field of 25 faces rendered at screen-size, this component's contribution would be difficult to distinguish from a distance.

## Symmetry

High symmetry (0.97). The cranial vault is bilaterally symmetric by nature.

## Safety

Clean through the full range. The cranial vault is smooth geometry with no folds or detail regions — it extrapolates cleanly.

## Current Usage

Listed in the stature axis in CLAUDE.md. A taller cranial vault contributes to an overall taller, leaner head shape (gaunt pole), while a flatter vault creates a more compact, stocky appearance (heavy pole). This is a reasonable application, though the visual impact at the scales used in the face field is likely minimal.

## Interactions

- With **beta1**: both affect vertical proportions, but differently. Beta1 stretches the entire face (including jaw, midface, and forehead). Beta6 specifically raises the cranial vault above the face. A face can be facially tall (beta1+) but with a flat vault (beta6-), creating a long-faced but compact-skulled individual. The combination gives independent control.
- With **beta4**: beta4 targets the forehead/brow region from the front (slope and ridge), while beta6 targets it from above (vault height). They affect the same general area but from different directions — one changes the angle, the other changes the height.

## Naturalist Notes

Cranial vault height varies across populations and correlates with overall head height. In forensic anthropology, cranial vault shape is used as one metric for ancestry estimation. The practical utility of this component for the Sarasti project is limited — it contributes to overall head silhouette but does not carry much semantic weight in terms of dominance, age, or emotion compared to jaw, brow, and midface components.
