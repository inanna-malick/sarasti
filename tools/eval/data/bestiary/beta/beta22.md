# Beta22 — Subtle Forehead Convexity

**Axis:** flatter forehead (-) ... more convex forehead (+)

## What It Does

Beta22 modulates the upper face — primarily forehead shape and possibly brow ridge depth. The effect is extremely subtle, requiring careful comparison of extreme renders to detect.

## Positive Pole (+1.5 to +3.0): Marginal Forehead Prominence

At +3.0 the forehead may appear very slightly more convex or the brow ridge fractionally deeper. The lower and midface are unchanged. At +1.5, no visible difference from neutral.

## Negative Pole (-1.5 to -3.0): Marginal Forehead Flattening

At -3.0 the forehead contour shifts by an almost imperceptible amount in the opposite direction. The effect is borderline noise.

## Symmetry

Appears symmetric (0.95). No left-right asymmetry detected, though the small magnitude makes this assessment uncertain.

## Safety

Extremely safe. No artifacts anywhere in the tested range.

## Current Usage

Not used in any recipe.

## Naturalist Notes

Beta22 likely captures variance in frontal bone morphology — the degree of forehead rounding or brow ridge projection that differs across the FLAME training population. This is a genuinely real anatomical axis of variation, but at component 22 the variance captured is too small to produce visually meaningful changes at normal magnitudes. Potentially useful as part of an identity hash but not for expressive binding.

**Confidence: low** — the effect may be partially confounded with lighting and viewing angle interpretation.
