# Beta20 — Subtle Midface Width

**Axis:** slightly wider midface (-) ... slightly narrower midface (+)

## What It Does

Beta20 is a very low-variance shape component that produces barely perceptible changes to midface width. It is deep enough in the PCA ordering that its effect at reasonable magnitudes (+/-3.0) is difficult to reliably distinguish from the neutral template.

## Positive Pole (+1.5 to +3.0): Marginal Midface Narrowing

At +3.0 there is a very subtle narrowing of the nose bridge and midface region, with the chin possibly tapering by a fraction. The effect at +1.5 is essentially invisible. One has to compare the -3.0 and +3.0 renders side by side to see any difference, and even then the change is marginal.

## Negative Pole (-1.5 to -3.0): Marginal Midface Widening

At -3.0 there is a barely detectable broadening of the nasal area and a very slight squaring of the lower midface. At -1.5, the render is indistinguishable from neutral to casual inspection.

## Symmetry

Appears symmetric (0.95) but the effect is so small that precise symmetry assessment is unreliable. The left34 and right34 views show no obvious left-right bias.

## Safety

Extremely safe. No artifacts at any tested value. The low variance of this component means vertices move very small distances even at large coefficient values.

## Current Usage

Not used in any recipe.

## Naturalist Notes

This is the territory where PCA components begin to capture very fine individual variation rather than gross morphological axes. Beta20 likely corresponds to subtle nasal bridge and perinasal width differences in the FLAME training population. Its utility for expressive recipes is minimal — the effect would be swamped by any of the lower-numbered components. Its value lies in identity differentiation, where small unique combinations of these high-numbered betas can make each face look subtly distinct without changing the overall character.

**Confidence: low** — the effect is so subtle that the description may be partially noise or lighting interpretation.
