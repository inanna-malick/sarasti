# Beta8 — Neck Thickness and Submental Mass

**Axis:** thin/defined neck (-) ... thick/full neck (+)

## What It Does

Beta8 targets the lowest region of the face model — the junction where the jaw meets the neck, the submental triangle (under the chin), and the neck column itself. This is the plumbing of the face: the structural base that everything else sits on top of.

## Positive Pole (+1.5 to +3.0): The Thick Neck

Pushing positive thickens the neck and fills the area under the chin. The neck column widens, the submental area gains mass (the "double chin zone" fills in, though not with a double chin specifically — more like the structural foundation thickens). The chin may advance slightly. At +1.5 the neck is subtly wider. At +3.0 the neck is visibly thick and the under-chin area is full — a stocky, robust neck-jaw base.

The 3/4 views are the most revealing angle for this component: the profile shows the neck-to-chin contour filling from a defined angle to a smoother, thicker curve.

## Negative Pole (-1.5 to -3.0): The Thin Neck

Pulling negative thins the neck and hollows the submental area. The neck column narrows, the under-chin transition becomes sharper and more defined, and the chin may recede slightly. At -1.5 the neck is subtly more slender. At -3.0 it is noticeably thin — the neck-to-jaw transition is crisp and the overall lower head reads as lean.

## Subtlety Level

This is among the subtler components because its primary region of effect (the neck and under-chin area) is not where viewers typically focus when reading a face. In a frontal view, much of the change is hidden behind the jaw itself. The 3/4 views make the effect more visible. At the typical rendering size of a face in the Sarasti field, this component's contribution would be difficult to read as anything other than a vague sense of "thicker" or "thinner" in the overall head base.

## Symmetry

Good symmetry (0.96). The neck thickens or thins evenly on both sides.

## Safety

Clean through the full range. The neck and submental geometry is relatively simple (smooth surfaces, no fine details) and extrapolates well.

## Current Usage

Listed in the stature axis in CLAUDE.md. Positive contributes to heaviness; negative to gauntness. This is appropriate — neck thickness is a body composition signal rather than a dominance structure signal.

## Interactions

- With **beta0**: beta0 thickens everything including the neck. Beta8 provides independent neck-specific mass control. You could have a large head (beta0+) on a thin neck (beta8-) for an unusual but structurally valid face.
- With **beta3**: an interesting pairing. Beta3 sharpens the jaw angle while beta8 fills the area below the jaw. Using both positive creates a face with a sharp jaw sitting on a thick neck — a powerful, wrestler-like architecture. Using beta3+ with beta8- creates a sharp jaw on a thin neck — more of a model or lean-athletic look.

## Naturalist Notes

Neck circumference is one of the strongest single-measurement predictors of body mass and muscular development. In the FLAME training data, this component likely correlates with body weight and overall physical build. The practical relevance for Sarasti is modest — the neck is visible but not a primary carrier of the crisis-state information that drives the face field. It serves mainly as a subtle background cue for the stature/build axis.
