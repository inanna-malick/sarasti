# Beta18 — Bone Structure Refinement

**Axis:** soft, padded contours (-) ... angular, sculpted bone structure (+)

## What It Does

Beta18 is a distributed angularity component that adjusts the structural definition of the entire face — cheekbone prominence, brow ridge sharpness, jaw angularity, and the crispness of transitions between facial planes. It is the most important "quality" component in the dominance recipe.

## Positive Pole (+1.5 to +3.0): The Sculpted Face

Pushing positive adds definition across the face. Cheekbones become more prominent, the brow ridge sharpens, the jaw gains angularity, and the transitions between forehead-brow-orbit and cheek-jaw become more crisp. The face reads as "chiseled" — good bone structure showing through. At +3.0 the sculpted quality is visible, though still subtle in isolation.

## Negative Pole (-1.5 to -3.0): The Soft Face

Pulling negative softens everything. Cheekbones recede, the brow ridge rounds, the jaw softens, and facial planes merge into gentler transitions. The face reads as more padded and less structurally defined. At -3.0 the face has a noticeably smoother, less angular quality.

## Symmetry

Good bilateral symmetry (0.95). Both 3/4 views are consistent mirror images.

## Safety

No artifacts at any tested value. The component is subtle per unit but is used at weight 3.75 in the dominance recipe — the joint highest weight alongside beta3 and beta23. At this amplified weight, it contributes meaningful structural change.

## Current Usage

Used in the **dominance** shape axis with weight 3.75 — the highest weight in the recipe, tied with beta3 and beta23. This makes beta18 one of the three most important contributors to the soyboi-chad shape axis. Positive dominance (chad) gets angular bone structure; negative (soyboi) gets soft, rounded contours.

## Interactions

- With **beta0** (global mass): beta0 adds volume, beta18 adds angularity. A massive face can be either soft (beta0+ beta18-) or angular (beta0+ beta18+). The dominance recipe uses both, producing faces that are both larger AND more angular.
- With **beta13** (midface mass): beta13 adds tissue volume, beta18 adds structural definition. They stack in the dominance recipe — the chad pole gets both more mass and more angularity.
- With **beta23** (bone detail): both are high-weighted bone structure components in the dominance recipe. They likely capture slightly different aspects of skeletal definition at different spatial frequencies.

## Naturalist Notes

Beta18's role in the dominance recipe is crucial even though it is subtle in isolation. The difference between a face that looks "dominant and strong" versus merely "large and swollen" is largely about bone structure showing through. Beta0 and beta3 provide the mass; beta18 (and beta23) provide the structural quality that makes that mass read as skeletal rather than adipose. Without beta18, the chad pole would look puffy. With it, the chad pole looks built. This is the "bone structure" component in the most literal sense — it is what makes a face look like good bones rather than just a big head.
