# Beta5 — Nose Length and Midface Vertical

**Axis:** short/upturned nose (-) ... long/drooping nose (+)

## What It Does

Beta5 is where the shape components begin to narrow their focus. Rather than reshaping the entire skull, beta5 hones in on the nose and its immediate surroundings — specifically, the vertical extent of the nasal structure and the philtrum region.

## Positive Pole (+1.5 to +3.0): The Long Nose

Pushing positive elongates the nose downward. The distance from nasal bridge to tip increases, the tip itself may drop slightly (angling more toward the upper lip), and the philtrum — the groove between nose and mouth — lengthens. The overall midface stretches vertically through the nasal corridor. At +1.5 this is a subtle lengthening. At +3.0 the nose is visibly longer and the midface reads as stretched through this region specifically.

The 3/4 views show the profile change: the nose projects further from the face and the tip drops lower relative to the alar base.

## Negative Pole (-1.5 to -3.0): The Short Nose

Pulling negative compresses the nose vertically. The tip lifts slightly (more upturned), the philtrum shortens, and the overall nose reads as more compact. At -1.5 the shortening is subtle. At -3.0 the nose is noticeably compact with a slightly retrousse character.

## Subtlety Level

This component is meaningfully subtler than beta0 through beta4. The changes are real and confirmed across the frontal progression, but they do not produce the dramatic whole-face transformation that the earlier components achieve. Careful side-by-side comparison is needed to fully appreciate the effect, especially in the front view. The 3/4 profile views make the change more apparent since nasal projection and drop are easier to read in silhouette.

## Symmetry

Good symmetry (0.96). Nasal changes are bilaterally even.

## Safety

Clean through the full range. The nose mesh holds together well even at extremes. No vertex bunching or folding.

## Current Usage

Referenced in the stature axis (CLAUDE.md documentation), not in dominance. This makes sense — nose length correlates more with age and vertical proportions than with structural dominance or mass.

## Interactions

- With **beta1**: beta1 stretches the whole face vertically; beta5 stretches the nose region specifically. Together they give two levels of control: global vertical proportions and nose-specific proportions. A face could have a tall overall shape (beta1+) but a short nose (beta5-), or vice versa.
- With **beta2**: beta2 is nose WIDTH; beta5 is nose LENGTH. Together they offer full 2D control over nasal proportions. Wide+long, wide+short, narrow+long, narrow+short — all four combinations are available by combining these two components.

## Naturalist Notes

The human nose continues to grow throughout life — cartilage, unlike bone, never stops. Noses get longer, wider, and droop more with age. Beta5 positive could therefore serve as a subtle age cue: longer noses reading as older faces. The nasal index (width/height ratio) is one of the most variable facial metrics across human populations, and beta5 combined with beta2 captures both dimensions of this variation.
