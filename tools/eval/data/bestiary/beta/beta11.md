# Beta11 — Forehead Contour / Cranial Vault

**Axis:** flat, sloped forehead (-) ... prominent, rounded forehead (+)

## What It Does

Beta11 reshapes the upper cranium, adjusting the roundness and forward projection of the forehead and the profile of the brow ridge. It operates on the cranial vault contour rather than its size.

## Positive Pole (+1.5 to +3.0): The Prominent Forehead

Pushing positive rounds the forehead and brings it slightly forward. The cranial vault becomes higher and more domed. The brow ridge softens marginally. At +3.0 the effect is subtle — a slightly more prominent, rounded upper skull profile.

## Negative Pole (-1.5 to -3.0): The Flat Forehead

Pulling negative flattens the forehead and creates a more sloped upper skull profile. The brow ridge becomes marginally more pronounced relative to the flattened forehead. At -3.0 the forehead is noticeably more recessed, though the change is modest.

## Symmetry

Good bilateral symmetry (0.95). The left and right 3/4 views confirm consistent behavior on both sides.

## Safety

No artifacts at any tested value. Extremely well-behaved. The visual change is barely perceptible in frontal view; slightly more visible in profile. This is a fine-tuning parameter.

## Current Usage

Not used in any current recipe.

## Interactions

- With **beta1** (vertical stretch): beta1 stretches the face vertically including forehead height; beta11 reshapes the forehead contour independent of overall elongation.
- With **beta4** (brow ridge): both can affect the brow region, but beta4 operates more dramatically while beta11 is subtler.

## Naturalist Notes

Beta11 is barely visible from the front. Its primary utility would be in profile views or 3/4 angles where forehead contour matters. For the current application (a 25-face grid viewed primarily from the front), this component's contribution would be nearly invisible. It is best suited for identity noise or for applications where profile silhouette matters.
