# Beta12 — Chin/Jaw Angularity

**Axis:** soft, rounded jaw (-) ... angular, squared jaw (+)

## What It Does

Beta12 fine-tunes the angularity of the lower face — chin prominence and jaw angle sharpness. Where beta3 dramatically changes jaw width, beta12 operates on the edge quality and shape of the chin.

## Positive Pole (+1.5 to +3.0): The Angular Jaw

Pushing positive makes the chin slightly more squared and prominent, and the mandibular angle becomes marginally sharper. At +3.0 the lower face reads as more structured and sculpted. The jaw line has more definition.

## Negative Pole (-1.5 to -3.0): The Soft Jaw

Pulling negative softens the chin and rounds the jaw angle. At -3.0 the lower face is noticeably less angular — the chin is rounder and the mandibular edge is smoother. The face reads as gentler in the lower third.

## Symmetry

Good bilateral symmetry (0.94). The left and right 3/4 views are consistent.

## Safety

No artifacts at any tested value. Clean geometry throughout. The effect is modest but most visible in the chin and jaw angle region.

## Current Usage

Not used in any current recipe.

## Interactions

- With **beta3** (jaw width): beta3 is the primary jaw axis and changes overall width dramatically. Beta12 fine-tunes the angular quality without changing width.
- With **beta2** (chin projection): beta2 is the primary chin projection axis; beta12 adds supplementary angularity detail.

## Naturalist Notes

Beta12 could be a useful supplement to the dominance recipe if more jaw-shape variation is desired beyond pure width. Currently, the recipe uses beta3 (width), beta16 (definition), and beta19 (chin depth), but not beta12. Its focus on jaw angularity independent of width makes it a potential "jaw quality" knob that could differentiate between faces that are equally wide but differ in edge sharpness.
