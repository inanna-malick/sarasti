# Beta27 — Subtle Cranial Vault Height

**Axis:** flatter skull (-) ... taller skull dome (+)

## What It Does

Beta27 appears to modulate the height or convexity of the cranial vault — the top of the skull. The face below the forehead is essentially unaffected.

## Positive Pole (+1.5 to +3.0): Marginal Skull Height

At +3.0 the top of the head may appear very slightly taller or more domed. The facial features — eyes, nose, mouth, jaw — are unchanged. At +1.5, no visible effect.

## Negative Pole (-1.5 to -3.0): Marginal Skull Flattening

At -3.0 the cranial vault may be fractionally lower. The face is unchanged. The effect is borderline detectable.

## Symmetry

Appears symmetric (0.95). Cranial vault modulation is inherently symmetric.

## Safety

Extremely safe. No artifacts at any value.

## Current Usage

Not used in any recipe.

## Naturalist Notes

The cranial vault is above the "face" per se, and its height variation contributes nothing to emotional or expressive reading. Beta27 captures real morphological variation — people do have different skull heights — but in a context where faces are viewed at small render scales for their expressive content, this axis is invisible and irrelevant. It would only matter if the project needed anatomically accurate head silhouettes.

**Confidence: low** — the characterization is tentative; the effect is very subtle.
