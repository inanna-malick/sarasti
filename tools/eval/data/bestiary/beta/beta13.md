# Beta13 — Midface Structural Mass

**Axis:** lean, narrow midface (-) ... fleshy, broad midface (+)

## What It Does

Beta13 adjusts the volume and structural mass of the mid-to-lower face region. It controls cheek fullness, zygomatic arch width, and the overall fleshiness of the face below the brow line. It is one of four components in the dominance recipe that are numbered beta13 or higher.

## Positive Pole (+1.5 to +3.0): The Full Midface

Pushing positive adds volume to the cheeks, broadens the zygomatic arches, and creates a face that reads as structurally full and well-built through the middle third. The nose may broaden marginally. At +3.0 the face has noticeably more midface mass — fuller cheeks, wider zygomatic region.

## Negative Pole (-1.5 to -3.0): The Lean Midface

Pulling negative strips volume from the cheeks and narrows the zygomatic region. The face reads as leaner and more angular, with less soft tissue padding over the midface bones. At -3.0 the face is noticeably thinner through the middle.

## Symmetry

Good bilateral symmetry (0.95). Both 3/4 views are consistent mirror images.

## Safety

No artifacts at any tested value. The effect is subtle per unit but becomes meaningful at the dominance recipe weight of 3.1, where it is multiplied by the dominance activation value.

## Current Usage

Used in the **dominance** shape axis with weight 3.1. Positive dominance (chad) adds midface structural mass; negative (soyboi) removes it.

## Interactions

- With **beta0** (global mass): beta0 scales everything; beta13 focuses the mass addition on the mid-to-lower face specifically.
- With **beta3** (jaw width): beta3 widens the jaw; beta13 adds cheek and midface volume above the jaw. Together they create a broad, full-featured lower-to-mid face.
- With **beta18** (bone structure): both participate in the dominance recipe. Beta13 adds soft tissue volume while beta18 refines angular structure. They are complementary — mass plus definition.

## Naturalist Notes

Beta13 at weight 3.1 in the dominance recipe likely captures the difference between faces with more subcutaneous fat and muscle mass in the midface region versus leaner faces. In the FLAME training population, this probably correlates with body composition and possibly sex. Its contribution to the dominance axis is appropriate — fuller midfaces read as more robust and physically powerful, while leaner midfaces read as more delicate.
