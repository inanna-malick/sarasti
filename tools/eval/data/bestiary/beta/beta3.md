# Beta3 — Jaw Definition and Mandibular Angularity

**Axis:** soft/recessed jaw (-) ... angular/projecting jaw (+)

## What It Does

Beta3 is the jaw component. Of the first 10 shape components, this one produces the most visually dramatic and immediately legible structural change. It controls the angularity, projection, and definition of the mandible and chin.

## Positive Pole (+1.5 to +3.0): The Hard Jaw

Pushing positive sharpens the lower face into angular definition. The mandibular angle (the corner of the jaw below the ear) becomes more pronounced and squared off. The chin projects forward and gains a more defined point or square shape. The jawline — the contour from ear to chin — transitions from a curve to a series of straighter lines with visible corners. At +1.5 this reads as "a person with a strong jaw." At +3.0 it becomes a conspicuously angular, forward-thrusting jaw — almost superhero-like in its structural definition.

The 3/4 views are particularly revealing: the gonial angle (where the ramus meets the body of the mandible) becomes a visible corner in the profile. The chin projects further forward from the neck plane. This is genuine bone geometry, not soft tissue.

## Negative Pole (-1.5 to -3.0): The Soft Jaw

Pulling negative dissolves the jaw's structural landmarks. The mandibular angle rounds off, the chin recedes and becomes less prominent, and the jawline smooths into a continuous curve from ear to chin. At -1.5 the face reads as soft-featured. At -3.0 the lower face is strikingly undefined — almost chinless, with the jaw blending smoothly into the neck without any angular interruption.

## Symmetry

High symmetry (0.96). The jaw changes are bilaterally even, as confirmed by the matching left and right 3/4 views.

## Safety

Clean through the full range. The jaw geometry is robust even at extremes — no mesh folding or vertex inversion. The mandible simply becomes more or less prominent without creating problematic geometry.

## Current Usage

This is the **highest-weighted component** in the dominance recipe at 3.75. It is the primary structural driver of the soyboi-to-chad transformation. This weighting is well-justified: jaw angularity and forward projection are among the strongest single-cue indicators of perceived dominance and physical formidability in the face perception literature.

## Interactions

- With **beta0**: beta0 adds mass to the whole head; beta3 specifically angularizes the jaw. A face with high beta0 and high beta3 is both massive AND angular — the "heavy, hard-jawed" archetype. High beta0 with low beta3 would be a large but soft-featured face.
- With **beta2**: beta2 widens the midface; beta3 sharpens the lower face. Together they create broad cheekbones flowing into a sharp jaw — a classic "dominant male face" template. The dominance recipe uses both (beta2 at 2.5, beta3 at 3.75).
- With **beta4**: beta4 also affects lower face structure. The two combine to give fine-grained control over jaw AND forehead simultaneously.

## Naturalist Notes

The jaw is perhaps the most sexually dimorphic and dominance-relevant facial structure in humans. Mandibular development is driven by testosterone during puberty — wider, more angular jaws are a secondary sexual characteristic associated with physical maturity and strength. Cross-cultural studies consistently show that jaw width and angularity are primary drivers of dominance perception.
