# Beta14 — Nose Length / Philtrum

**Axis:** short, retracted nose (-) ... long, projected nose (+)

## What It Does

Beta14 makes subtle adjustments to nose length, nasal tip projection, and the vertical proportions of the philtrum region. It is a fine-detail component that operates in the central lower-midface.

## Positive Pole (+1.5 to +3.0): The Long Nose

Pushing positive slightly lengthens the nose and projects the nasal tip forward. The philtrum elongates marginally and the upper lip may thin slightly. At +3.0 the nose is subtly longer but the change requires deliberate comparison to notice.

## Negative Pole (-1.5 to -3.0): The Short Nose

Pulling negative shortens the nose and retracts the nasal tip. The philtrum compresses and the upper lip becomes slightly fuller. At -3.0 the nose is marginally shorter and more compact.

## Symmetry

Good bilateral symmetry (0.95). No lateral bias visible in the 3/4 views.

## Safety

No artifacts at any tested value. The visual change between extremes is barely perceptible without side-by-side comparison. Extremely safe at any weight.

## Current Usage

Not used in any current recipe.

## Interactions

- With **beta10** (nasal bridge width): beta10 adjusts nasal bridge width while beta14 adjusts nose length and projection. These are orthogonal aspects of nasal shape and could be combined for comprehensive nose variation.

## Naturalist Notes

This is deep in the PCA tail where individual components capture progressively less variance. Beta14's visual impact is quite modest even at extremes. For the current face-field application where faces are viewed at small sizes in a grid, this component's contribution would be invisible. Its primary utility is as identity noise or in close-up views where nasal detail matters.
