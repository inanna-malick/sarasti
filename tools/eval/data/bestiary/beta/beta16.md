# Beta16 — Jaw Line Definition

**Axis:** soft, undefined jaw (-) ... sharp, defined jaw (+)

## What It Does

Beta16 controls the sharpness and definition of the jaw line — the edge quality of the mandibular contour. It operates on jaw angularity and chin squareness without significantly changing jaw width.

## Positive Pole (+1.5 to +3.0): The Defined Jaw

Pushing positive sharpens the jaw line. The mandibular angle becomes more acute and defined, the chin gains squareness, and the lower face reads as more structured and sculpted. At +3.0 the jaw line has noticeably more edge definition, though the effect is still modest in absolute terms.

## Negative Pole (-1.5 to -3.0): The Soft Jaw

Pulling negative softens the jaw line. The mandibular angle rounds, the chin loses squareness, and the lower face reads as smoother and less angular. At -3.0 the jaw line is notably less defined — the transition from jaw to neck becomes gentler.

## Symmetry

Good bilateral symmetry (0.94). Left and right 3/4 views are consistent.

## Safety

No artifacts at any tested value. Clean geometry throughout. Most visible in the jawline region when comparing extremes.

## Current Usage

Used in the **dominance** shape axis with weight 1.9. Positive dominance (chad) adds jaw definition; negative (soyboi) softens it.

## Interactions

- With **beta3** (jaw width): beta3 changes how wide the jaw is. Beta16 changes how sharp its edges are. A wide jaw can be either soft (beta3+ beta16-) or angular (beta3+ beta16+).
- With **beta12** (chin angularity): beta12 adjusts chin shape and jaw angle; beta16 adjusts jaw line sharpness. Both operate on the "quality" rather than "quantity" of the jaw.
- With **beta13** (midface volume): beta13 adds mass, beta16 adds definition. In the dominance recipe, they work together to produce a jaw that is both substantial and well-defined.

## Naturalist Notes

Beta16 at weight 1.9 in the dominance recipe is a supporting player rather than a lead. Its contribution is subtle but important — it prevents the chad pole from looking merely swollen (which pure mass from beta0/beta3 could produce) by adding structural edge quality. The difference between a dominant face and merely a fat face is partly about this kind of definition, and beta16 provides it.
