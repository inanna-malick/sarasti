# Beta21 — Lateral Skull / Ear Position

**Axis:** ear/temporal shift A (-) ... ear/temporal shift B (+)

## What It Does

Beta21 is a very low-variance component that primarily affects the lateral skull contour and ear region. Its effect is nearly invisible from the front and requires 3/4 views to detect any change at all.

## Positive Pole (+1.5 to +3.0): Subtle Ear/Temporal Shift

At +3.0 the ears and temporal region show a barely perceptible change in position or protrusion. The frontal face — nose, mouth, eyes, brow — is essentially unchanged. At +1.5, the effect is invisible.

## Negative Pole (-1.5 to -3.0): Opposite Ear/Temporal Shift

At -3.0 the lateral skull contour changes by a marginal amount in the opposite direction. Frontal appearance is unchanged.

## Symmetry

Appears symmetric (0.93), though the effect is too subtle for precise assessment. Both 3/4 views show comparable changes on their respective sides.

## Safety

Extremely safe. The vertex displacements are too small to produce any artifacts even at extreme values.

## Current Usage

Not used in any recipe.

## Naturalist Notes

Beta21 likely captures variance in ear morphology, temporal bone shape, or lateral skull contour that exists in the FLAME training population. From a frontal view — which is how the crisis monitor displays faces — this component is essentially invisible. It could serve as part of an identity hash (small random combinations of high betas to make faces look distinct) but has no value for expressive axes.

**Confidence: low** — the effect is at the threshold of visual detection at these magnitudes.
