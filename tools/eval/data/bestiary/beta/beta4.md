# Beta4 — Forehead Slope and Brow Ridge

**Axis:** flat/vertical forehead (-) ... projecting/heavy brow (+)

## What It Does

Beta4 governs the architecture of the upper face — the forehead's slope, the prominence of the brow ridge, and the heaviness of the supraorbital region. It is the upper-face counterpart to beta3's lower-face jaw definition.

## Positive Pole (+1.5 to +3.0): The Heavy Brow

Pushing positive develops the brow ridge and tilts the forehead forward. The supraorbital torus (the bony shelf above the eyes) thickens and projects, creating a more shadowed, deeper-set orbital area. The forehead slopes rather than standing vertical. The nose bridge may also gain some mass where it meets the brow. At +1.5 this reads as a face with a prominent brow — mature and heavy-featured. At +3.0 the brow ridge becomes the dominant architectural feature of the upper face, overhanging the eyes noticeably.

The 3/4 views reveal the forehead slope most clearly — the profile shows the forehead angling forward from the hairline to a prominent brow shelf, then dropping back to the eyes. This is a distinctly robust cranial architecture.

## Negative Pole (-1.5 to -3.0): The Clean Forehead

Pulling negative flattens the brow ridge and makes the forehead more vertical. The supraorbital area smooths into a gentle curve rather than a shelf. The eyes are less deeply set because there is less brow overhanging them. At -1.5 the face reads as lighter and younger in its upper structure. At -3.0 the forehead is strikingly flat and vertical — almost childlike in its lack of brow development.

## Symmetry

High symmetry (0.97). The brow ridge develops evenly on both sides.

## Safety

Clean through the full range. The brow ridge geometry is stable even at +3.0. No artifacts in any view.

## Current Usage

Used in the **dominance** recipe with weight 1.9 — a moderate contribution that adds brow heaviness to the chad pole. This is appropriate as a secondary dominance cue; the brow ridge adds to the overall impression of structural robustness without being as dramatic as the jaw (beta3 at 3.75).

## Interactions

- With **beta3**: the essential pairing. Beta3 builds the jaw; beta4 builds the brow. Together they create a face that is structurally heavy from top to bottom — the full dominance package. The current recipe uses both.
- With **beta0**: beta0 adds global mass, beta4 adds specifically upper-face mass. On a face already enlarged by beta0, beta4 ensures the brow keeps up with the jaw in terms of heaviness.
- With **beta7**: beta7 affects the orbital area from around the eye. Combined with beta4's brow ridge, they control the overall depth and shadowing of the eye socket.

## Naturalist Notes

Brow ridge prominence is another sexually dimorphic feature influenced by testosterone during development. The supraorbital torus is one of the key features used in forensic anthropology to estimate biological sex from skeletal remains. In living faces, a heavy brow reads as mature, robust, and dominant. Beta4 capturing this axis cleanly makes it a natural complement to beta3 in any dominance or masculinity recipe.
