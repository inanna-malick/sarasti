# Beta29 — Subtle Neck / Submental Variation

**Axis:** (indeterminate) (-) ... (indeterminate) (+)

## What It Does

Beta29 produces changes at or below the threshold of reliable visual detection. The most likely affected region is the neck or submental (under-chin) area, but confidence is very low.

## Positive Pole (+1.5 to +3.0): Imperceptible

At +3.0, there may be a marginal change in neck thickness or the contour under the chin. The effect cannot be reliably confirmed. At +1.5, identical to neutral.

## Negative Pole (-1.5 to -3.0): Imperceptible

At -3.0, equally subtle. The face is functionally identical to neutral.

## Symmetry

Appears symmetric (0.95). No asymmetry detected.

## Safety

Extremely safe. Negligible vertex displacement at all tested values.

## Current Usage

Not used in any recipe.

## Naturalist Notes

Beta29 marks the end of the beta20s batch and exemplifies the general pattern for this range: each component captures real but increasingly fine population variation, with visual effects that fall below the detection threshold at +/-3.0 magnitudes. These high-numbered components are the morphological "fingerprint dust" of the FLAME model — they make each face unique in subtle ways that are important for photorealistic reconstruction but irrelevant for the crisis monitor's cartoon-level expressive faces.

The contrast with beta0 is instructive: beta0 produces a dramatic change at +/-1.5. By beta29, even +/-3.0 produces nothing visible. This is the expected behavior of PCA — each successive component captures less variance. The practical implication is that for expressive binding, the project should continue using components from roughly the first 50 betas and only reach for higher-numbered ones when it needs identity variation rather than expressive power.

**Confidence: low** — the anatomical characterization is speculative.
