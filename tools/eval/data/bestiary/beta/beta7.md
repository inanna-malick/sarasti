# Beta7 — Orbital Depth and Eye Socket Sculpting

**Axis:** shallow/flat orbits (-) ... deep-set/sculpted orbits (+)

## What It Does

Beta7 carves out the eye sockets. It controls the depth at which the eyes sit within the skull and the definition of the bone structure that frames them. This is the first component in the beta0-beta9 range that primarily targets the periorbital region.

## Positive Pole (+1.5 to +3.0): The Deep-Set Eyes

Pushing positive deepens the eye sockets. The orbital rims — the bony ridges that frame the eyes above, below, and to the sides — become more pronounced, creating a more sculpted frame around each eye. The eyes themselves sit further back from the plane of the face. The upper cheeks may hollow slightly as the sub-orbital bone structure becomes more defined.

The visual result is a face with more intense, shadowed eyes. The deeper the sockets, the more shadow collects under the brow ridge and along the orbital margins, giving the gaze a more piercing, concentrated quality. At +1.5 this reads as "deep-set eyes." At +3.0 the orbital sculpting is dramatic — almost gaunt in the eye region, with pronounced shadowing.

## Negative Pole (-1.5 to -3.0): The Flat-Set Eyes

Pulling negative flattens the orbital area. The eyes sit closer to the surface plane of the face, the orbital rims recede, and the upper cheeks fill out. The overall periorbital region becomes smoother and less sculpted. At -1.5 the eyes look slightly more prominent (less recessed). At -3.0 the eye region is noticeably flat — the eyes are almost on the same plane as the cheeks and brow, creating a less intense, more open appearance.

## Subtlety Level

Moderate subtlety. The orbital depth change is real and visible, but it manifests primarily through shadow behavior rather than through dramatic contour changes. In the rendered images, the change between -3.0 and +3.0 is clear upon comparison but not as immediately striking as beta0 (mass) or beta3 (jaw). The 3/4 views help by showing the depth in profile.

## Symmetry

Good symmetry (0.96). Both eye sockets deepen or flatten together.

## Safety

Clean through the full range. The orbital geometry is well-behaved. No eyelid clipping or vertex folding at any tested value.

## Current Usage

Used in the **dominance** recipe with weight 1.25 — the lightest touch in the recipe. This adds a subtle dose of orbital depth and intensity to the chad pole: deep-set eyes under a heavy brow (beta4) create the classic "intense stare" that reads as dominant and formidable. The weight is appropriately restrained — too much orbital deepening would push from "intense" to "gaunt/skeletal," which is more of a stature effect than a dominance one.

## Interactions

- With **beta4** (brow ridge): the essential pairing for eye appearance. Beta4 builds the shelf above the eyes; beta7 carves out the depth below that shelf. Together they determine how deeply hooded and intensely shadowed the eyes appear. The dominance recipe uses both (beta4 at 1.9, beta7 at 1.25).
- With **beta0**: on a face already enlarged by beta0, the orbital deepening from beta7 creates more dramatic shadow because there is more brow mass casting shadow into a deeper socket.
- With **beta3** (jaw): not directly interacting, but on a face with both a sharp jaw (beta3+) and deep-set eyes (beta7+), the overall impression is one of stark, angular intensity throughout the face.

## Naturalist Notes

Orbital depth is influenced by both bone structure and soft tissue. Deeply set eyes are associated with lower body fat percentage (the sub-orbital fat pad thins, revealing more bone structure) and with older age (as the orbital fat pad atrophies). In dominance perception, deeply set eyes read as more intense partly because the shadow makes the eye appear smaller relative to the socket, creating a more focused, concentrated gaze. Beta7 captures this axis reasonably cleanly, though it also carries some upper-cheek hollowing that blurs the line between "deep-set eyes" and "gaunt cheeks."
