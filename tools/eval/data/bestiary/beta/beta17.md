# Beta17 — Periorbital Region / Eye Socket Shape

**Axis:** flat, undefined orbital region (-) ... defined, structured orbital region (+)

## What It Does

Beta17 reshapes the periorbital region — the eye sockets, orbital ridges, and the transition zone between the eyes and the upper cheeks. It is a fine-detail component that operates on a small, specific facial region.

## Positive Pole (+1.5 to +3.0): The Defined Orbit

Pushing positive makes the orbital ridges slightly more pronounced, fills out the upper cheek region subtly, and creates a more defined eye-socket-to-cheek transition. At +3.0 the eye sockets appear marginally more structured.

## Negative Pole (-1.5 to -3.0): The Flat Orbit

Pulling negative flattens the orbital region. The orbital ridges soften, the upper cheek area becomes slightly more concave, and the transition between eye socket and cheek becomes less defined. At -3.0 the area around the eyes is noticeably smoother.

## Symmetry

Good bilateral symmetry (0.94). Both 3/4 views are consistent.

## Safety

No artifacts at any tested value. Minimal visual impact even at extremes. This is a fine-detail parameter deep in the PCA hierarchy.

## Current Usage

Not used in any current recipe.

## Interactions

- With **beta4** (brow ridge): beta4 is the primary brow ridge/upper face axis. Beta17 provides finer periorbital detail below and adjacent to the brow.
- With **beta7** (mid-face width): beta7 affects broader mid-face width while beta17 is more focused specifically on the eye socket region.

## Naturalist Notes

Beta17 is one of the subtlest components in this batch. Its visual impact is nearly invisible from the front at standard viewing distances. Its primary utility would be in close-up views or as identity noise. For the 25-face grid application, this component is unlikely to contribute meaningful visual variation. It could become relevant if the application ever supports detail views or face zoom interactions.
