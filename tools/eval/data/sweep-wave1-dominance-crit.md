# Art Director Critique: Dominance Axis Sweep (Wave 1)

This document evaluates the SHAPE axis for DOMINANCE in the Hormuz visualization. The goal is for a trader to instantly recognize market momentum through the bone structure of the face renders.

## Image Evaluation

### dominance_-1.0_front.png (Submissive/Soyboi Pole)
This face clearly reads as submissive and "retreating." The jaw is extremely narrow and the chin is delicate, creating a triangular face shape that signals a lack of physical or metaphorical power. The neck is thin, almost fragile, which reinforces the "weak momentum" read. The cranial vault appears somewhat elongated and tall, which, combined with the narrow jaw, gives an "intellectual but powerless" or "childlike" impression. 

The structural change is highly readable. The transition from the midface to the jaw is very steep. One minor artifact is that the cranial vault height (β7) might be pushing the proportions slightly into "uncanny" territory, making the head look a bit too tall relative to the narrow base.

### dominance_-0.5_front.png
A successful intermediate step. The face is still recognizably "weak" compared to the neutral, but the proportions are more grounded. The narrowing of the jaw is distinct from the 0.0 level but doesn't feel as extreme as the -1.0 pole. The neck thickness is appropriately reduced. It reads as a "timid" or "conservative" instrument.

### dominance_0.0_front.png (Neutral)
The baseline for the sweep. Proportions are balanced and "average." It serves as a solid anchor point, allowing the deviations at both poles to be measured effectively by the eye.

### dominance_0.5_front.png
The face begins to broaden. The jaw squareness (β4) is starting to manifest, and the brow ridge (β2) is becoming more defined. There is a noticeable "widening" of the neck and midface. The instrument feels more "stable" and "established" than the neutral face. The change is distinct and moves the character toward a more masculine, assertive archetype.

### dominance_1.0_front.png (Dominant/Chad Pole)
This pole is a strong success in terms of readability. The jaw is wide and squarer, the brow ridge is heavy and assertive, and the neck is thick, suggesting "strong momentum" and "brute force." The face is compact and dense. The shadow cast by the brow ridge over the eyes adds a level of intensity that perfectly captures the "dominant" persona.

The face looks like a completely different person compared to the -1.0 pole, which is exactly what we want. However, the ears appear a bit "pushed out" by the wider jaw/neck structure, which might need a subtle adjustment to keep them from looking tacked on. The jawline is very aggressive—perhaps right at the edge of being too "blocky," but for a financial "Chad," it works.

## Recommendations

Overall, the current recipe is performing well, but we can refine it to increase the "instant readability" and fix minor proportional issues.

*   **Jaw Width (β4):** Increase weight from 1.5 to **1.8**. While the current Chad is good, we want that jaw to be the primary signal. A slightly wider anchor will make the "assertive" read even more immediate.
*   **Cranial Vault (β7):** Reduce the absolute weight from -1.5 to **-1.2**. At the -1.0 pole, the head looks a bit too tall/egg-shaped. Dialing this back will keep the "submissive" read without looking unnatural.
*   **Neck Thickness:** If we have a component that specifically controls neck girth independent of the jaw, we should increase it slightly for the positive pole. The current thickness is good but could be even more "bullish."
*   **Ear Positioning:** Check if there's a β component for ear "pinning" or depth. The dominant faces would benefit from ears that stay closer to the head as the jaw widens, preventing the "Dumbo" effect.
*   **Periorbital Depth (β23):** The current -0.8 is good, but consider moving to **-1.0** for the Chad pole. Deeper-set eyes under that heavy brow will really sell the "focused/aggressive" market momentum.
