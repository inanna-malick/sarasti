# Facial Expression Perception Study: Blind Axis Analysis (Wave 16c)

## Neutral Baseline
The neutral baseline (neutral_front.png, neutral_closeup.png) shows a bald male face with a relaxed, symmetrical expression. Lips are closed, eyes are open, and brows are in a neutral position.

---

## Axis A
**a) Description:**
- **Positive Pole:** Mouth is wide open, showing a dark interior. The jaw is significantly dropped. Lips appear tense, slightly pulled back at the corners. Eyes and brows remain relatively neutral, though there may be slight tension in the lower eyelids.
- **Negative Pole:** Mouth is closed. Lips are pressed together and slightly widened/upturned at the corners, creating a "tight-lipped" or suppressed expression.

**b) Top 3 Guesses:**
1. **Surprise ↔ Determination** (Confidence: High)
2. **Shock ↔ Suppression** (Confidence: Medium)
3. **Vocalization ↔ Silence** (Confidence: Low)

**c) Readability Score: 4**
The open mouth in the positive pole is very clear, and the contrast with the tight-lipped negative pole is significant.

**d) What's Missing:**
Adding brow movement (raising for surprise, lowering for determination) would significantly clarify the intent.

---

## Axis B
**a) Description:**
- **Positive Pole:** Mouth is slightly open with rounded, "pouty" lips (O-shape). Eyebrows are slightly raised, and the overall look is one of uncertainty or mild questioning.
- **Negative Pole:** Mouth is open wider than the positive pole, with a dropped jaw. The upper lip is lifted slightly, and the lower lip is pulled down. It resembles a gasp or a state of awe/shock.

**b) Top 3 Guesses:**
1. **Pout ↔ Gasp** (Confidence: High)
2. **Uncertainty ↔ Awe** (Confidence: Medium)
3. **Tentative ↔ Emphatic** (Confidence: Low)

**c) Readability Score: 3**
While the poles are distinct, they both involve an open mouth, which can be confusing at a distance. The difference in lip shape is the primary differentiator.

**d) What's Missing:**
More pronounced brow and eye involvement (widening eyes for the negative pole, knitting brows for the positive pole) would help.

---

## Axis C
**a) Description:**
- **Positive Pole:** Mouth is closed. Lips appear slightly thinned and the jaw looks a bit more clenched or defined. The gaze feels more intense, possibly due to a slight forward head tilt.
- **Negative Pole:** Mouth is slightly open in a relaxed, almost slack-jawed way. The head is tilted slightly back/side, suggesting a lack of focus.

**b) Top 3 Guesses:**
1. **Concentration ↔ Relaxation** (Confidence: High)
2. **Hostility ↔ Submission** (Confidence: Medium)
3. **Focus ↔ Distraction** (Confidence: Low)

**c) Readability Score: 2**
The differences are very subtle. Without the closeup, it's difficult to distinguish these from the neutral baseline at first glance.

**d) What's Missing:**
Increased tension in the mid-face (nasolabial folds) for the positive pole and more obvious relaxation/droop in the eyelids for the negative pole.

---

## Axis D
**a) Description:**
- **Positive Pole:** Mouth is closed but strikingly asymmetrical. The character's left (viewer's right) corner of the mouth is pulled up and back, creating a smirk.
- **Negative Pole:** Mouth is closed and appears very similar to the neutral baseline, perhaps slightly more compressed or "flat."

**b) Top 3 Guesses:**
1. **Smirk ↔ Neutrality** (Confidence: High)
2. **Contempt ↔ Respect** (Confidence: Medium)
3. **Irony ↔ Sincerity** (Confidence: Low)

**c) Readability Score: 4**
The asymmetry of the smirk is a very strong, instantly recognizable signal.

**d) What's Missing:**
A slight squint in the eye on the smirking side would make the expression much more convincing and "alive."

---

## Comparison & Overlap
- **Axis A & B:** Both utilize open-mouth configurations. There is a risk of overlap if the jaw drop in A is not clearly distinguished from the gasp/pout in B.
- **Axis C Similarity:** Axis C is so subtle that it might be perceived as "neutral" unless contrasted directly with the other axes.
- **Uniqueness:** Axis D is the most unique due to its strong asymmetry, making it stand out from the otherwise mostly symmetrical axes.
