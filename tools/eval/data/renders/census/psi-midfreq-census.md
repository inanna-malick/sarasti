# FLAME Expression Component Census (ψ13 - ψ29)

This document catalogues the visual effects of mid-frequency PCA components in the FLAME expression space.

## ψ13: Skepticism / Subtle Engagement
- **Negative pole (-3.0)**: Mouth corners pulled down, nostrils flared. Expression: Skeptical, displeased.
- **Positive pole (+3.0)**: Mouth corners slightly elevated, fuller cheeks. Expression: Subtle smirk, neutral-positive engagement.
- **Gradient quality**: Very smooth.
- **Primary face region**: Lower face (mouth/cheeks).
- **Symmetry**: Bilateral.
- **Usefulness rating**: 3/5.
- **What it could add**: Subtle attitude or "side-eye" skepticism vs. calm acceptance.

## ψ14: Vitality / Exhaustion
- **Negative pole (-3.0)**: Narrowed upper lip, pronounced nasolabial folds, sunken eyes. Expression: Stern, aged, or weary.
- **Positive pole (+3.0)**: Fuller upper lip, smoother mid-face, alert eyes. Expression: Youthful, relaxed, attentive.
- **Gradient quality**: Smooth.
- **Primary face region**: Mid-face.
- **Symmetry**: Bilateral.
- **Usefulness rating**: 2/5.
- **What it could add**: Modulating "alertness" or "exhaustion" without changing core emotion.

## ψ15: Temperament (Toughness / Vulnerability)
- **Negative pole (-3.0)**: Wider jaw, square lower face, flat lips. Expression: Determined, tough, slightly aggressive.
- **Positive pole (+3.0)**: Narrower face, pointy chin, narrow mouth. Expression: Delicate, sensitive, slightly anxious.
- **Gradient quality**: Smooth.
- **Primary face region**: Lower face (jawline/mouth).
- **Symmetry**: Bilateral.
- **Usefulness rating**: 4/5.
- **What it could add**: Essential for defining the "character" of the individual—stable vs. vulnerable.

## ψ16: Focus / Intensity

- **Positive pole (+3.0)**: Lowered/pulled-together brows, squinted eyes. Expression: Concentrated, intense, slightly angry.

- **Primary face region**: Upper face (brows/eyes).
- **Symmetry**: Bilateral.
- **Usefulness rating**: 5/5.
- **What it could add**: Clear way to add intensity or focus, perfect for emergency scenarios.
