## Component Details

### β20: Philtrum Length / Mouth Vertical Position
*   **Negative Pole (-3.0):** The mouth is pulled upward, significantly shortening the distance between the nose and the upper lip (philtrum). This creates a sense of tension or "pinched" alertness.
*   **Positive Pole (+3.0):** The mouth is positioned lower on the face, lengthening the philtrum. This gives a more relaxed, "long-faced," or slightly lethargic appearance.
*   **Multi-Angle Insights:** The `left34` view reveals how the mouth vertical shift affects the overall profile balance. `Closeup` is essential for observing the compression of the philtrum ridges.
*   **Smoothness:** 5/5
*   **Label:** Philtrum Length
*   **Artifacts:** None.

### β21: Alar Width (Nostril Flare)
*   **Negative Pole (-3.0):** The nostrils (alae) are narrowed and pulled inward, giving the nose a sharper, more delicate, and "discerning" appearance.
*   **Positive Pole (+3.0):** The nostrils are widened and flared outward. This increases the "robustness" of the nose and can suggest a more "sensual" or earthy character type.
*   **Multi-Angle Insights:** The `front` view shows the width change, but `left34` and `closeup` reveal the actual curvature and flare of the alar base, which is missed in 2D.
*   **Smoothness:** 5/5
*   **Label:** Alar Width / Nostril Flare
*   **Artifacts:** None.

### β22: Chin Shape (Rounded vs. Squared)
*   **Negative Pole (-3.0):** The chin is smooth, narrow, and rounded. It contributes to a softer, more approachable, or "youthful" jawline.
*   **Positive Pole (+3.0):** The chin becomes wider, more angular, and develops a slight central depression (cleft/split chin). This adds "grit" or "determination" to the character.
*   **Multi-Angle Insights:** `Left34` is critical; it shows the transition from a smooth curve to a more blocky, projecting chin structure that `front` flattens.
*   **Smoothness:** 5/5
*   **Label:** Chin Angularity / Cleft
*   **Artifacts:** None.

### β23: Zygomatic Width (Cheekbone Prominence)
*   **Negative Pole (-3.0):** The cheekbones are narrow and recessed, making the midface appear slender or even gaunt.
*   **Positive Pole (+3.0):** The zygomatic arches flare outward, widening the face at the level of the eyes. This creates a "sturdier" or more conventional facial structure.
*   **Multi-Angle Insights:** `Left34` highlights the lateral protrusion of the bone, which defines the "high cheekbone" look. `Front` only shows a change in head width.
*   **Smoothness:** 5/5
*   **Label:** Zygomatic Width
*   **Artifacts:** None.

### β24: Philtrum / Maturity (Aging Axis)
*   **Negative Pole (-3.0):** Similar to β20 but with a more specific focus on the "pout" and vertical compactness. Highly youthful and innocent.
*   **Positive Pole (+3.0):** Elongates the midface and mouth area in a way that suggests tissue sagging associated with aging. Sullen and "heavy."
*   **Multi-Angle Insights:** `Closeup` reveals subtle changes in lip volume and philtrum depth that are key to the "aging" effect.
*   **Smoothness:** 5/5
*   **Label:** Philtrum Maturity
*   **Artifacts:** None.

### β25: Forehead Height
*   **Negative Pole (-3.0):** The hairline is lower, and the brow is vertically compressed. Can appear "primitive" or "low-brow."
*   **Positive Pole (+3.0):** The forehead is taller and more expansive. Suggests a "high-brow" or "intellectual" character type.
*   **Multi-Angle Insights:** `Left34` shows the dome of the cranium and how the hairline shift alters the perceived volume of the head.
*   **Smoothness:** 5/5
*   **Label:** Forehead Height
*   **Artifacts:** None.

### β26: Mouth Corner Tilt (Sentiment)
*   **Negative Pole (-3.0):** The corners of the mouth are slightly upturned at rest. This creates a "naturally" optimistic or amiable baseline disposition.
*   **Positive Pole (+3.0):** The corners are slightly downturned. This creates a cynical, discontented, or "bearish" baseline expression.
*   **Multi-Angle Insights:** `Closeup` is vital to see if this is a mesh-based "smile" (shape) vs. an expression. It appears to be a structural structural tilt of the commissures.
*   **Smoothness:** 5/5
*   **Label:** Mouth Corner Disposition
*   **Artifacts:** None.

### β27: Ocular Projection (Eye Depth)
*   **Negative Pole (-3.0):** The eyes are deep-set within the orbits, with the brow ridge projecting further forward. Suggests focus, skepticism, or "intensity."
*   **Positive Pole (+3.0):** The eyes are more prominent and forward-facing, with a shallower brow ridge. Suggests naivety, astonishment, or openness.
*   **Multi-Angle Insights:** `Left34` is the only view that clearly shows the relative projection of the eyeball vs. the brow. `Front` only perceives this as a change in eye size.
*   **Smoothness:** 5/5
*   **Label:** Ocular Projection / Brow Depth
*   **Artifacts:** None.

### β28: Jaw Definition (Angularity)
*   **Negative Pole (-3.0):** The mandibular angle is sharp and well-defined. Gives a "strict," Relentless, or "highly-trained" look.
*   **Positive Pole (+3.0):** The jawline is softer, more blurred, and blends more into the neck. Gives a "lenient," "soft," or "unformed" appearance.
*   **Multi-Angle Insights:** `Left34` shows the "hook" of the jawbone, which is the primary marker for this component.
*   **Smoothness:** 5/5
*   **Label:** Jaw Definition
*   **Artifacts:** None.

### β29: Midface Vertical Scale
*   **Negative Pole (-3.0):** Vertically compresses the region between the eyes and the mouth. Creates a "short-faced," alert, or "juvenile" look.
*   **Positive Pole (+3.0):** Vertically stretches the midface. Creates a "long-faced," mature, or "melancholic" look.
*   **Multi-Angle Insights:** `Left34` reveals how the stretching of the midface affects the nose-to-chin ratio in profile.
*   **Smoothness:** 5/5
*   **Label:** Midface Vertical Scale
*   **Artifacts:** None.

## Signal vs. Noise Assessment

At this range (β20-β29), the components remain remarkably clean "signals." While they are more subtle than the primary axes (β0-β9), they all correspond to well-recognized anatomical variations.

*   **β24** and **β29** are strong for "Asset Maturity" (Aging).
*   **β28** is excellent for "Institutional Rigidity" (Sharp vs. Soft regulation).
