## Review Questions

### 1. Most Similar Faces
**alarm_-1.5.png** and **aggression_-1.5.png**. 
Both look like "polite, slightly forced smiles." They are confusable because the system seems to map negative values on both axes to a similar pro-social/pleasant muscle configuration that doesn't clearly distinguish between "lack of alarm" and "lack of aggression."

### 2. Least Changed from Neutral
**aggression_-0.5.png**. 
The shift is extremely subtle, with only a slight softening of the eyes. What's missing is a more distinct "relaxation" signal—perhaps a more pronounced drop in the mouth corners or a visible easing of the brow tension—to differentiate it from the absolute baseline.

### 3. Most Compelling/Readable
**aggression_1.5.png**. 
The combination of the tightened jaw and the downward-driving brow creates a visceral sense of mounting hostility. It works because it captures the physiological "fight" response with high fidelity and zero ambiguity.

### 4. Unsettling or Uncanny Faces
**alarm_1.5.png** is particularly unsettling. 
The extreme dilation of the mouth paired with the wide, fixed stare creates a "death mask" effect. The dark void of the mouth feels detached from the rest of the facial structure, triggering a strong uncanny valley response.

### 5. Casting Call: "Person Watching Their Portfolio"

**Top 3 (The Winners):**
1. **fatigue_1.0.png** - Perfectly captures the weary, critical grind of a long professional review.
2. **alarm_0.5.png** - The ideal "wait, did I just find a bug?" expression.
3. **aggression_0.5.png** - Captures the sharp, low-level irritation of spotting a design flaw you've fixed three times already.

**Bottom 3 (The Rejections):**
1. **alarm_-2.0.png** - Way too jubilant; they look like they just won the lottery, not like they're reviewing work.
2. **aggression_-2.0.png** - Too submissive; it looks like they are apologizing to the screen.
3. **alarm_1.5.png** - Too histrionic; it looks like the portfolio is literally haunted and they are about to scream.
