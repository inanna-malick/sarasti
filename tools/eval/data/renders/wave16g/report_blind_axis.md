# Blind Facial Expression Analysis Report

## Neutral Baseline
The neutral baseline shows a relaxed face with no visible emotional signaling. The mouth is closed and level, eyes are naturally open, and eyebrows are in a resting position. This serves as a solid "zero" point for all subsequent axes.

---

## Axis A
- **Positive Pole (`pos`)**: The face conveys a sense of **distress, sadness, or mild shock**. The mouth is slightly open with corners down-turned. The eyes appear slightly wider than neutral, suggesting concern or "taking in" bad news.
- **Negative Pole (`neg`)**: The face conveys **happiness, warmth, and contentment**. There is a clear, closed-mouth smile. The cheeks are slightly elevated, and the eyes have a softer, more engaged look.
- **Top 3 Guesses**:
  1. Valence (Negative to Positive)
  2. Happiness vs. Sadness
  3. Contentment vs. Distress
- **Readability**: 4/5. Both poles are distinct and easily identifiable as "good" vs "bad" emotions.
- **Difference between poles**: 5/5. They represent opposite ends of the emotional spectrum.
- **Notes**: The positive pole (distress) feels slightly artificial in the way the mouth hangs open, but it is clear in intent.

---

## Axis B
- **Positive Pole (`pos`)**: The face conveys **intense concentration, anger, or sternness**. The eyebrows are sharply lowered and pulled together. The mouth is tightly compressed.
- **Negative Pole (`neg`)**: The face conveys **shock, surprise, or panic**. The eyebrows are high, eyes are wide, and the mouth is open in a "gasp" or "shout" shape.
- **Top 3 Guesses**:
  1. Arousal (High vs Low Intensity or Surprised vs Angry)
  2. Surprise vs. Anger
  3. Alertness vs. Focus
- **Readability**: 5/5. These are the most distinct and "iconic" expressions in the set.
- **Difference between poles**: 5/5. Complete reversal of eyebrow and mouth posture.
- **Notes**: The inside of the mouth in the negative pole is very dark/flat, which looks a bit artificial (the "void" effect).

---

## Axis C
- **Positive Pole (`pos`)**: The face conveys **skepticism, determination, or mild annoyance**. The lips are pursed and flattened, and the gaze seems more focused/narrowed.
- **Negative Pole (`neg`)**: The face conveys **disgust, physical discomfort, or contempt**. The mouth is slightly open and asymmetric, as if reacting to an unpleasant smell or thought.
- **Top 3 Guesses**:
  1. Disgust vs. Skepticism
  2. Contempt vs. Determination
  3. Disagreeableness vs. Focus
- **Readability**: 3/5. The positive pole is subtle and could be interpreted as simple concentration. The negative pole is clearly "negative" but the specific emotion (disgust vs pain) is slightly blurred.
- **Difference between poles**: 4/5. They are both "unpleasant" or "serious" but the physical deformation is quite different.
- **Notes**: The asymmetry in the negative pole is well-done and adds to the "disgust" feeling.

---

## Axis D
- **Positive Pole (`pos`)**: The face conveys **irony, smugness, or a "knowing" smirk**. One corner of the mouth is pulled up and out, while the rest of the face remains relatively neutral.
- **Negative Pole (`neg`)**: This pole is **nearly indistinguishable from neutral**. It might be a tiny bit more relaxed or "blank" than the baseline, but the change is minimal.
- **Top 3 Guesses**:
  1. Contempt/Smugness vs. Neutrality
  2. Irony vs. Apathy
  3. Social Signaling vs. Resting State
- **Readability**: 4/5 for the positive pole; it's a very clear smirk. The negative pole's readability is low because it looks neutral.
- **Difference between poles**: 3/5. Since one pole is neutral, the axis feels more like a "0 to 1" intensity slider rather than a bi-directional spectrum.
- **Notes**: The smirk is very natural-looking.
