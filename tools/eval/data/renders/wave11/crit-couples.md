## Clinical Analysis

### 1. Emotional Legibility
The most legible faces—the ones I could read before they even sat down—are the high-intensity pure states: **`aggressive_1.0`**, **`alarmed_1.0`**, **`wired_1.0`**, and **`exhausted_1.5`**. These are faces where the nervous system has taken over completely, and there is no social masking left.

### 2. Curiosity Inducers
The faces that would make me lean forward are the "split" presentations where the eyes and mouth are telling different stories. Specifically, **`cross_euphoric-aggressive`** (joy in hostility) and **`cross_smirk-alarmed`** (panic beneath pride). These represent the most complex psychological conflicts. **`full_euphoric-exhausted-yielding`** also stands out as it looks like a "forced peace" or traumatic bonding.

### 3. Confusion Pairs
- **`yielding_1.0`** and **`exhausted_0.5`**: Both present as passive, but one is an active choice to open up while the other is a failure of energy. Confusing them would lead to a major therapeutic error.
- **`wired_0.5`** and **`alarmed_0.5`**: Both look like "general anxiety." Without higher intensity, it's hard to tell if the patient is hyper-focused (wired) or startled (alarmed).

### 4. The "Smirk" Spectrum
- **`smirk_1.0`**: This is **performing confidence**. It's a wall meant to keep the therapist out.
- **`cross_smirk-alarmed`**: This is **masking insecurity**. The smirk is a shield that has just been cracked.
- **`smirk_0.5`**: This feels like **genuine amusement**, specifically of the derisive kind. It's the most subtle and potentially the most damaging in a couple's session.

### 5. Euphoria vs. Happiness
- **`euphoric_0.8`** is the only one that looks like **genuine connection**.
- The higher intensities (**1.2 and up**) look like someone **trying to convince themselves** they're okay, or moving into a manic state. 
- **`full_puffy-soyboi-euphoric`** is the most performative; it looks like a person terrified of conflict trying to "smile" their way into safety.
