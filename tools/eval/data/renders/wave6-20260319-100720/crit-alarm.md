# Art Director Evaluation: ALARM Axis
Timestamp: 2026-03-19T10:07Z

## 1. Artifact Threshold
The gradient remains geometrically stable throughout the ±3.0 range, with no visible mesh tearing or clipping. However, "uncanny valley" distortions begin to manifest at the extremes:

*   **Alarmed Pole (+):** The face starts looking "wrong" at **+2.0**. The mouth opening becomes increasingly rectangular and unnatural, losing the organic curvature expected in human anatomy. By **+3.0**, the upper lip is pulled so high that the nasolabial folds become unnaturally sharp, and the interior of the mouth is a flat, dark void that lacks anatomical depth. The eyes also reach a point of "staring" that feels more like a frozen mask than a reactive expression.
*   **Euphoric Pole (-):** The threshold for "uncanny" is around **-2.5**. At **-3.0**, the eye squinting is so extreme that the eyes are reduced to mere slits, which looks more like a physical squint against bright light or a "smug" contraction rather than elation. The mouth is pulled into a very thin, tight line that feels constricted rather than expressive of joy.

## 2. Gradient Quality
The progression from neutral (0.0) to both poles is exceptionally smooth and monotonic. There are no sudden jumps in intensity or values where the face reverts to a more neutral state.

The "sweet spot" for this axis is the **±1.5 range**. At these values, the expressions read clearly and remain within the realm of believable human emotion. Beyond 1.5, the intensity ramps into caricature. The transition from 0.0 to 1.0 is particularly effective for subtle dashboard states, while 1.5 provides the necessary "alarm" without breaking immersion.

## 3. Recipe Quality
*   **Alarmed Pole (+):** Currently, this reads more as **"shocked surprise"** than "alarm." To make it more "alarmed" (in the sense of fear or crisis), the recipe needs more tension in the brow. Specifically, the inner brows should be pulled together and upward (the corrugator and frontalis medialis muscles), creating "worry lines" in the forehead. The current raised-brow look is too "clean" for a crisis state.
*   **Euphoric Pole (-):** This reads as **"smug satisfaction"** or "internalized glee." To achieve true "euphoria," the recipe should incorporate an open-mouthed smile. Euphoria is an expansive emotion; the current closed-mouth, squinty-eyed look feels too guarded and constricted. Incorporating more "lift" in the cheeks (zygomaticus major) and allowing the mouth to open slightly would better communicate elation.
