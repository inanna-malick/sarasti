# Dominance Axis Evaluation: Wave 6 (2026-03-19)

## 1. Artifact Threshold
The dominance axis demonstrates asymmetrical stability. The positive pole (dominant) is significantly more robust than the negative pole (submissive).

*   **Negative Pole (-1.5 to -3.0):** The face starts looking "wrong" at **-1.5**. The primary failure point is the **neck-to-skull transition**. The neck becomes unnaturally thin and elongated, losing its anatomical connection to the jawline. By **-2.0**, the lower face appears compressed and skeletal, as if the bone structure is collapsing inward. At **-3.0**, the proportions are impossible—the head looks like a lightbulb on a stick, moving into "alien" or "mutant" territory.
*   **Positive Pole (+2.5 to +3.0):** This pole holds its geometric integrity much longer. Even at **+3.0**, the face remains recognizably human, albeit an extreme "heavy-set" or "bruiser" archetype. The "break" here is less about geometry and more about **caricature**. The jaw becomes so wide that it starts to look like an ogre, and the neck becomes a solid block with the head, losing the definition of the throat.

## 2. Gradient Quality
The gradient is smooth and monotonic. There are no sudden jumps in geometry between the 0.5-unit steps.

*   **Noticeability:** The shift from neutral (0.0) is subtle but perceptible at **+/- 0.5**. At this value, the face simply looks "slimmer" or "sturdier" without changing its identity.
*   **Sweet Spot:** The range of **-1.0 to +1.5** is the ideal operating zone. Within this window, the dominance read is clear and effective for character differentiation without crossing into uncanny or distorted proportions. Values beyond this should be used sparingly for "extreme" personality types.

## 3. Character Quality
The structural changes successfully communicate the intended traits.

*   **Dominant Pole (+):** Reads strongly as **"powerful/authoritative"**. The wide jaw and thick neck are the primary "chad" markers. The prominent brow adds a sense of weight and seriousness. It looks like a classic "powerhouse" CEO or a military commander. What’s missing is perhaps a bit more refinement in the mid-face; the dominance change is very bottom-heavy.
*   **Submissive Pole (-):** Reads as **"delicate/refined"** at -1.0, but quickly slides into "weak/gaunt" beyond that. The narrow jaw and thin neck sell the lack of physical presence. At -1.0, it looks like a sensitive intellectual; at -3.0, it looks malnourished or sickly.

**Character Diversity:**

1.  **The Gaunt/Mutant** (-3.0 to -2.0): Uncanny, distorted.

3.  **The Baseline** (0.0): Neutral.
4.  **The Capable Professional** (+0.5 to +1.5): Stronger jaw, more presence.
5.  **The Powerhouse** (+2.0 to +3.0): Physically imposing, blocky.
