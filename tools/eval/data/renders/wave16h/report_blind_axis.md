# Blind Facial Expression Analysis - Wave 16h

**Date:** Thursday, March 19, 2026
**Researcher:** Gemini CLI (Blind Test Protocol)

## Methodology
The researcher examined 4 expression axes (A, B, C, D) across 3 camera angles (front, left34, closeup) plus a neutral baseline. The researcher was not informed of the intended semantic meaning of the axes prior to analysis.

---

## Baseline: Neutral
The neutral face shows a bald male with a calm, blank expression. Eyes are focused forward, mouth is closed and straight.

---

## Axis A
### Descriptions
*   **Positive Pole:** Mouth slightly open, eyes slightly wider/tense. Conveys "alarmed surprise" or "active attention".
*   **Negative Pole:** Wide, clear smile. Eyes slightly narrowed. Conveys "happiness", "joy", or "amusement".

### Analysis
*   **Top 3 Guesses:**
    1. Valence (Negative to Positive)
    2. Joy / Happiness
    3. Surprised vs. Happy
*   **Readability:** 4/5
*   **Pole Difference:** 5/5
*   **Notes:** The smile is very natural. The "positive" pole (which looks negative/tense) is a bit uncanny.

---

## Axis B
### Descriptions
*   **Positive Pole:** Mouth corners turned down, eyes look "heavy" or "sad". Conveys "sadness", "disappointment", or "pouting".
*   **Negative Pole:** Mouth open in a snarl/shout, brow ridge looks tense. Conveys "anger", "rage", or "aggressive disgust".

### Analysis
*   **Top 3 Guesses:**
    1. Sadness vs. Anger
    2. Submission vs. Dominance
    3. Distress vs. Aggression
*   **Readability:** 5/5
*   **Pole Difference:** 5/5
*   **Notes:** Both poles are extremely distinct. The anger is very readable, though the dark mouth interior is a bit artificial.

---

## Axis C
### Descriptions
*   **Positive Pole:** Mouth tightly compressed, eyes narrowed in focus. Conveys "determination", "concentration", or "intensity".
*   **Negative Pole:** Slight head tilt, mouth slightly slack, eyes look unfocused. Conveys "confusion", "uncertainty", or "bewilderment".

### Analysis
*   **Top 3 Guesses:**
    1. Concentration vs. Confusion
    2. Certainty vs. Uncertainty
    3. Arousal/Intensity (High vs. Low)
*   **Readability:** 3/5
*   **Pole Difference:** 4/5
*   **Notes:** More subtle than the first two. The head tilt on the negative pole is a very effective cue for confusion.

---

## Axis D
### Descriptions
*   **Positive Pole:** Asymmetrical smirk/sneer, mouth pulled to one side. Conveys "contempt", "sarcasm", or "disdain".
*   **Negative Pole:** Nearly identical to neutral. Conveys "indifference" or "flat affect".

### Analysis
*   **Top 3 Guesses:**
    1. Contempt / Sarcasm
    2. Asymmetry (Smirk)
    3. Arrogance vs. Neutrality
*   **Readability:** 4/5 (for the smirk)
*   **Pole Difference:** 3/5 (due to one pole being neutral)
*   **Notes:** Effective asymmetrical expression.

---

## Overall Summary
The model (Wave 16h) shows high readability for primary emotions (Joy, Anger, Sadness) and good success with complex states like Confusion and Contempt.

**Strongest Axis:** Axis B (Sadness vs. Anger) - very clear emotional contrast.
**Weakest Axis:** Axis C (Concentration vs. Confusion) - somewhat subtle and harder to distinguish from neutral at a glance.
