# Scenario Re-evaluation: Divergence (Comparative)

**Previous Score**: 9/10  
**New Score**: 10/10

## Comparison to Previous Issues

### 1. Synchronicity (Timing Jitter)
- **Before**: All faces transitioned at the exact same rate, leading to a mechanical "synchronized swimming" feel.
- **After**: Subtle random jitter is clearly visible, especially at t=10 and t=20. Faces in the same group now hit their emotional peaks at slightly different times, creating a far more organic and believable crowd effect.
- **Result**: **Resolved.**

### 2. d5 Stature ("The Torn")
- **Before**: d5 was at "normal" stature, which looked fine but didn't fully communicate its "caught between" narrative state.
- **After**: d5 now exhibits a "fatigued" stature. At t=44, it is visibly smaller and more "slumped" than the surrounding Winners (titans), yet remains larger than the Losers (sprites). This intermediate size perfectly reinforces its role as the outlier.
- **Result**: **Resolved.**

### 3. Occlusion/Spacing
- **Before**: Winners grew so large they threatened to overlap, potentially muddling the silhouettes.
- **After**: While the Winners still feel massive and "inflate" to fill the frame, the spacing at t=40 and t=44 has been refined. There is no actual occlusion between the titan faces, maintaining a high-impact but clean visual composition.
- **Result**: **Resolved.**

## Regressions & New Observations
- **No regressions noted.** 
- The contrast between the euphoric, warm-flushed "Winners" and the distressed, pallid "Losers" remains the strongest element of the scenario.
- The oscillation of d5 at t=44 (appearing worried while neighbors are euphoric) is even more effective with the new stature adjustment.

## Final Verdict
The scenario has moved from a high-quality technical demo to a polished piece of visual storytelling. The small adjustments to timing and stature have added the necessary "soul" to the crowd simulation.
