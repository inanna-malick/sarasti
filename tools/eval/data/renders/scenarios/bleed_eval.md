# Scenario Evaluation: Slow Bleed (90s Market Decline)

## Overview
The "Slow Bleed" scenario successfully communicates a collective drift from contentment to resignation. The 4x4 grid creates a powerful sense of "unanimous horror" as the group slowly loses vitality and hope.

## Criteria Evaluation

1. **Gradual decline**: 
   - The decline reads as very gradual. There are no jarring jumps between frames. 
   - The transition from t00 to t30 handles the loss of the initial "market high" smiles well.
   - t60 to t75 feels slightly stagnant; the fatigue levels are very similar in these two frames.

2. **Unanimity vs variety**: 
   - Excellent convergence. By t88, the entire grid shares a heavy, exhausted aesthetic.
   - Variety is maintained through differing head orientations and slightly offset timings in expression shifts.

3. **Canary (b0, top-left)**: 
   - **Confirmed.** At t15, b0 shows early signs of jaw tension and eye strain while the rest of the grid remains relatively stable. By t30, b0 has fully transitioned to a "worry" state.

4. **Holdout (b15, bottom-right)**: 
   - **Confirmed.** b15 maintains its initial smile through t15 and only begins to fade at t30. It is the last to lose its "contentment" highlights.

5. **Micro-rallies (t=45)**: 
   - **Subtle.** I did not observe a clear "uptick" in positivity/contentment. 
   - Instead, the "rally" at t45 manifests as increased **alertness** in some faces (e.g., b5 shifting gaze from down-left to up-center). It feels more like "desperate searching" than a recovery.

6. **Fatigue accumulation**: 
   - Very effective. The skin tone shifts from a warm, vibrant peach at t00 to a gray, pallid, and shadowy texture by t88. The "hollowing out" of the eyes is particularly noticeable.

7. **Gaze network**: 
   - Clearly visible. Early frames show faces looking "at" each other or toward the viewer. Late frames (t75, t88) show a collective downward gaze, selling the "bottom of the market" resignation.

8. **Stature shrinkage**: 
   - Visible but subtle. The heads seem to slump lower in their respective cells rather than physically scaling down, which effectively conveys a loss of "presence."

9. **Head tilt**: 
   - Very clear. The gradual downward tilt across the grid is one of the strongest indicators of the transition to resignation.

## Overall Score: 8.5/10
The emotional readability is high. The unanimity of the 16 faces creates a haunting "choir of despair" effect that perfectly fits the "Slow Bleed" theme.

## Top 3 Issues / Suggestions
1. **Pronounce the t45 Micro-Rally**: If a "dead cat bounce" is intended, a few faces (especially the holdouts) should briefly regain a flash of their t00 skin highlights or a slight mouth-corner lift to contrast with the subsequent final collapse.
2. **Distinct t60-t75 Transition**: These two frames are too similar. Adding a more pronounced "stature drop" or a specific "eye-closing" (fatigue) increment at t75 would help maintain the sense of continuous decline.
3. **Sweat/Sheen for "Wired" phase**: In the mid-decline (t30-t45), adding a slight oily sheen to the skin could sell the "high-stress/wired" phase before the final "matte/exhausted" resignation sets in.
