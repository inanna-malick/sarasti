# Scenario Re-Evaluation: Crash (60s Market Narrative)

## Comparative Analysis of Top 3 Issues

### 1. Abrupt Haven Pivot (t=25 → t=35)
- **Status:** **Unchanged.**
- **Analysis:** The transition for the Haven group (bottom-right 2x2) remains extremely sharp. In the 10-second gap between the t=25 "Peak Panic" and t=35 "Rally," the faces jump from maximum distress to confident smiles with no intermediate "relief" or "realization" state visible in the provided stills. This still feels like a binary state-switch rather than a physical transition.
- **Recommendation:** Introduce a t=30 frame to capture the "turn."

### 2. Sentinel Specificity (t=55)
- **Status:** **Improved.**
- **Analysis:** The sentinels (top-left cluster) are now more clearly distinguished from the "exhausted" herd in the final frame. While both groups are smaller in scale, the sentinels at t=55 now exhibit a "defeated" gaze—looking down and away (left)—whereas the herd maintains a neutral, forward-facing glazed expression. This subtle change effectively communicates their unique narrative arc of "failed vigilance."
- **Regression:** None detected.

### 3. Panic Saturation (t=25)
- **Status:** **Mostly Unchanged.**
- **Analysis:** At the peak of the crash (t=25), mouth shapes are still highly uniform across the 16 faces. While there is minor variation in aperture size (e.g., Index 0 is slightly more "O" shaped than the wide gasp of Index 15), they are all variants of the "open mouth gasp." The previously requested "clenched-jaw" panic variety is still missing, which maintains a slightly mechanical feel during the peak.
- **Recommendation:** Inject 2-3 "teeth-clenched" or "grimace" expressions into the herd at t=25.

## Overall Assessment
The scenario remains a powerful demonstration of the engine's ability to render complex, multi-agent narratives. The "Wavefront" effect at t=18 is still world-class in its readability. The specific fix for sentinel gaze at t=55 adds a layer of sophistication to the ending.

**New Overall Score: 9.2/10** (Up from 9/10)
