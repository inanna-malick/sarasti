# Scenario Evaluation: False Calm (30s)

## 1. THE SNAP CONTRAST (t=24 vs t=25.5)
The contrast is **exceptional**. At t=24, the entire grid is in a state of placid, slightly smiling boredom. At t=25.5, the "snap" is immediately legible. 
- **fc0 (top-left)**: Transitions from calm to full-blown panic (dilated pupils, jaw drop, skin tone shift).
- **Neighbors**: The panic is visibly "infecting" the nearest neighbors (1,0 and 0,1), while the rest of the herd remains oblivious. This creates a powerful focal point that draws the eye exactly where the action starts.

## 2. Calm period (t=0–24)
The calm period is stable and effectively boring. 
- **Micro-movements**: There is enough subtle idle motion to keep the faces from looking like static statues, but not enough to break the "boredom" theme.
- **Consistency**: All 16 faces maintain a unified mood throughout this phase.

## 3. Wavefront gradient (t=27)
The Manhattan distance radiation is clearly visible. 
- **Top-left (Early Snappers)**: These faces are already deep into the panic cycle, showing more extreme distortion and "spent" energy.
- **Bottom-right (The Unaware)**: Faces at (3,3) and (3,2) are still relatively calm, creating a beautiful diagonal "tension line" across the grid. The gradient feels physical, like a pressure wave.

## 4. Mid-transition faces (t=29)
By t=29, the bottom-right corner is caught in the most effective part of the animation: the **uncanny transition**. 
- These faces are partially calm and partially panicked—eyes beginning to bulge while the mouth is still in a neutral pose. This is significantly more "horror" than the full panic of the sentinel, as it captures the moment of realization.

## 5. fc0 foreshadowing (t=10, t=20)
- **t=10**: No obvious foreshadowing; looks identical to the herd.
- **t=20**: Subtle micro-tension is visible in the brow of fc0. It's not enough to alert a casual observer, but upon re-watch, the "sentinel" is clearly vibrating with a different frequency than the others.

## 6. Flush/color during snap
The pallor wave is a highlight. As faces snap, they lose their warm, saturated skin tones, replaced by a cold, desaturated grey-purple tint. This "blood draining from the face" effect reinforces the wavefront.

## 7. Stature during snap
There is a noticeable "hunkering" effect. Panicked faces seem to retract their necks or shrink slightly in the frame, shifting from an "open/curious" posture to a "closed/defensive" one.

## 8. Overall score: 9/10
The transition is violent, sudden, and perfectly paced. The contrast between the 24 seconds of boredom and the sudden 1-second snap is the strongest technical achievement of this scenario.

## 9. Top 3 issues
1. **Mouth Clipping**: During the most extreme jaw drops (e.g., fc0 at t=27), there is some minor texture stretching/clipping near the corners of the mouth.
2. **Eye Convergence**: A few of the "mid-transition" faces have slightly divergent eyes that look more "broken" than "panicked."
3. **Shadow Consistency**: The shadow cast by the heads on the imaginary "floor" or background doesn't seem to react to the hunkering movement, making the heads feel slightly detached from the space during the snap.
