# Scenario Evaluation: Crash (60s Market Narrative)

## 1. Narrative Arc
The emotional progression is highly readable and follows the intended arc:
- **t=00 to t=05 (Calm):** The grid is stable with relaxed, slightly positive expressions.
- **t=12 (Detection):** Excellent "sentinel" effect. The top-left face shows sharp shock/alarm while the rest remain oblivious.
- **t=18 (Spread):** A clear wavefront of panic is visible moving diagonally from top-left to bottom-right.
- **t=25 (Peak):** System-wide distress. All faces show high-intensity negative affect (fear/panic).
- **t=35 (Divergence):** The most striking moment. The "Havens" group (bottom-right) breaks away from the panic, shifting to positive expressions and warmer tones.
- **t=45 to t=55 (Exhaustion/Resignation):** The herd settles into a "low-energy" distress (sadness/exhaustion), while sentinels appear physically diminished (smaller scale).

## 2. Group Differentiation
- **t=25:** Differentiation is intentionally low as the "dip" affects everyone, though the top-left faces (sentinels) show a slightly more "exhausted" panic compared to the fresh shock of the bottom-right.
- **t=35:** Extremely high differentiation. Havens are distinctly happy/warm/large, while the Herd remains in a state of grimacing distress.

## 3. Wavefront
The panic onset is clearly graduated. At t=12, only (0,0) is shocked. By t=18, the top two rows are panicked while the bottom-right quadrant is still transitioning from calm to concern.

## 4. Haven Rally
Confirmed. At t=35 and beyond, the bottom-right 2x2 cluster (Indices 10, 11, 14, 15) shows:
- **Expression:** Shifts from panic (t=25) to a confident smile.
- **Color:** Shifts from ashen/grey to warm/saturated orange tones.
- **Scale:** Visibly larger than the surrounding "Herd" faces.

## 5. Stature Changes
- **Sentinels:** Start at standard or slightly larger scale. By t=55, they are noticeably smaller, conveying a sense of "shrunken" or "defeated" status.
- **Havens:** Grow significantly in scale during the rally at t=35+, dominating the bottom-right of the frame.

## 6. Flush/Color
- **Pallor:** t=25 shows a successful "drained" look across the grid—desaturated and slightly blue/grey.
- **Warmth:** Havens at t=35+ have a distinct "golden hour" glow that contrasts sharply with the "cold" look of the exhausted herd.

## 7. Per-face Variety
The herd faces are sufficiently distinct. While they share the same emotional state, their underlying identities (eye color, jaw shape, brow structure) are different enough to avoid a "clone army" effect.

## 8. Overall Score: 9/10
The scenario successfully tells a complex data story (market crash with varying group reactions) using only facial semantics. The timing of the wavefront and the clarity of the haven divergence are particularly strong.

## 9. Top 3 Issues
1. **Abrupt Haven Pivot:** The transition for havens between t=25 (panic) and t=35 (joy) is very fast. A "relief" or "realization" frame in between might smooth the narrative.
2. **Sentinel Specificity:** At t=55, the sentinels look very similar to the "exhausted" herd, just smaller. A more "resigned/defeated" expression (looking down or away) could further distinguish their role.
3. **Panic Saturation:** At t=25, the mouths across the herd are very similar. Slightly more variety in the "mouth-open" vs "mouth-clenched" panic would add realism.
