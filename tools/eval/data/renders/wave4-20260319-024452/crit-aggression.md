# Art Director Critique: Aggression Axis (Wave 4)

## 1. DOES AGGRESSION READ?
Aggression successfully distinguishes itself from the existing "Alarm" axis by shifting from a reactive, shocked state to a proactive, directed one. While Alarm is defined by wide-eyed "deer in headlights" vulnerability, Aggression +1.0 introduces a focused, narrowing of the gaze and a tightening of the mid-face. The key difference is the *intent*: the aggressive face looks like it is hunting a price level, whereas the alarmed face looks like it is being hunted by one. The sustain is there; it feels like a persistent state of combat rather than a momentary flinch.

## 2. THE AGGRESSIVE POLE
The +1.0 pole captures a "predatory focus" well, but it stops just short of the "desk-slamming rage" requested. It currently reads more as intense concentration or a simmering threat. To push this into true financial-crisis rage, we need more "messy" tension: specifically, more nostril flare and a more pronounced snarl or lip curl that exposes some dental tension. The narrowed eyes are excellent—they are sharp and "locked on"—but the lower half of the face needs to convey the verbal "scream" or the clenched-jaw "grit" of someone losing a million dollars a minute.

## 3. THE YIELDING POLE
The yielding pole (-1.0) successfully conveys a softening of the features, moving away from the "Neutral" (0.0) state into something more submissive. However, it risks feeling "blank" or merely "slightly sad." To differentiate it from "Exhaustion" (which should be characterized by heavy, slack lids and a lack of muscle tone), "Yielding" should feel like an active retreat. It looks like someone "giving up the fight," but could be improved by a more pronounced downward curve of the mouth corners and perhaps a slightly more "averted" gaze if the rig allows for a subtle eye-offset.

## 4. CROSS-AXIS MAGIC
This is where the axis truly shines. The compositions are highly recognizable:
*   **Aggressive + Alarmed (Berserker):** This is a standout. The wide-eyed shock combined with the aggressive brow creates a "manic" energy that perfectly captures market insanity.
*   **Aggressive + Wired (Predator):** Extremely effective. It looks like a high-frequency trader on hour 20 of a stimulant-fueled binge.
*   **Aggressive + Chad (Warlord):** The combination of dominant bone structure with aggressive tension creates a very convincing "master of the universe" in a fury.
*   **Aggressive + Soyboi (Cornered Rat):** This works surprisingly well. The lack of dominant structure makes the aggression feel desperate and dangerous—the "last lash out" of a failing fund.
*   **Yielding + Exhausted (Total Surrender):** This is the "liquidation" face. Total loss of hope.

## 5. WHAT'S MISSING?
To make this read as "RAGE" at a glance on a dashboard, we need to lean into the secondary physical markers of anger. Specifically: 
1. **Nostril Flare:** Essential for that "breathing fire" look.
2. **Nasolabial Depth:** Deepening the folds around the mouth to show tension.
3. **Neck Tension:** If possible, showing the "straining" of the neck muscles to sell the idea of a sustained scream or shout.
Currently, the skin looks a bit too relaxed for someone in a state of high aggression. We need to see the effort of the rage.
