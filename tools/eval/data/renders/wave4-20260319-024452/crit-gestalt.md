# Wave 4 Creative Director Critique: The Aggression Factor

## 1. DASHBOARD GLANCE (2 seconds)
With the introduction of the AGGRESSION axis and the nonlinear activation curve, the dashboard has finally stopped looking like a collection of static mannequins and started looking like a trading floor in cardiac arrest. Out of the ~25 faces, I’d say 8-10 now "POP" with enough intensity to grab a user's attention from across the room—specifically the high-aggression/high-alarm crossovers. The others successfully recede into "wallpaper" status, which is exactly what we want for stable assets. 

**Visual Dynamic Range: 8.5/10.** (Wave 3 was a sleepy 6/10). The nonlinear curve is the unsung hero here; the faces at 0.2 intensity actually look *concerned* now instead of just having a slight eye-twitch.

## 2. DISTINCT EMOTION COUNT
I can identify roughly 9 genuinely different psychological states now:
1. **The Deer in Headlights** (Pure Alarm)
2. **The Walking Dead** (Pure Fatigue)
3. **The Predator** (Pure Aggression) - *NEW*
4. **The Berserker** (Aggression + Alarm + Wired) - *NEW*
5. **The Smug Winner** (Aggression + Euphoria/Chad) - *NEW*
6. **The Manic Gambler** (Euphoria + Wired)
7. **The Defeated** (Yielding + Exhausted) - *NEW*
8. **The Focused Professional** (Low Alarm + Low Aggression)
9. **The Total Meltdown** (High Alarm + High Fatigue)

Aggression hasn't just added a new flavor; it has unlocked "Predatory" vs "Prey" dynamics that were completely missing in Wave 3.

## 3. DOES AGGRESSION BELONG?
It fits. It doesn't look like a DLC pack bolted onto a base game. The way the brow lowers and the mouth corners tighten in the aggression sweep mirrors the anatomical logic of the alarm and fatigue axes. It feels like it's pulling on the same "muscle groups" of the underlying FLAME model. It is stylistically coherent because it focuses on *tension*—the core currency of this dashboard.

## 4. THE 3-AXIS CUBE
The vocabulary is significantly richer. "Berserker Panic" (Aggressive + Alarmed + Wired) is a terrifyingly specific state that perfectly captures a market participant who is losing money and lashing out. However, we are approaching the "muddled" event horizon. At 0.4 intensities, the distinction between "Aggressive-Exhausted" and "Yielding-Alarmed" starts to blur. We’re pushing the limits of what a human can decode in a 2-second glance. We don't need a 4th axis; we need to sharpen the 3 we have.

## 5. SINGLE BIGGEST REMAINING PROBLEM
**The "Yielding" (-1.0 Aggression) ambiguity.** Right now, being "Yielding" looks too much like being "Tired." If a user sees a yielding face, they might misinterpret it as fatigue. We need to find a way to make "Yielding" feel like *active submission* or *acceptance*—perhaps through a slight head tilt or a specific softening of the eyes that doesn't just look like drooping eyelids.

## 6. WHAT'S STILL MISSING?
**Disbelief/Shock.** Our "Alarm" is very active—it's a "run for your life" alarm. We are missing the "I can't believe the bank just collapsed" shock, which is usually characterized by more facial rigidity and less mouth movement. Also, **Contempt.** In a crisis, there’s a lot of "I told you so." A micro-expression for contempt would add that final layer of Wall Street toxicity we’re aiming for.
