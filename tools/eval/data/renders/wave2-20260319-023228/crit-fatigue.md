# Art Director's Critique: Fatigue Axis & Emotional Quadrants
**Date:** 2026-03-19
**Batch:** wave2-20260319-023228

## SET 1 — THE FATIGUE GRADIENT (EXHAUSTED ↔ WIRED)

Looking at the 7-image arc, the gradient is functional but remains dangerously subtle for its intended use. The transition from Neutral (0.0) to Exhausted (-1.0) is more readable than the move toward Wired (+1.0). The positive side of the axis (Wired) plateaus early; there is very little perceived difference between 0.7 and 1.0. At a glance, a user might mistake the "Wired" face for mere "Attentiveness" and the "Exhausted" face for "Boredom." We need to push the extremes much further to ensure these states are instantly recognizable in a high-density dashboard.

### The WIRED Pole (+1.0)
Currently, this face looks "alert" or "surprised," but it lacks the edge of a 3 AM crisis. To make this feel like someone running on adrenaline and caffeine scanning 12 monitors:
- **Eye Intensity:** The eyes shouldn't just be "wide"; the lower lids need a slight "squinch" or tension to indicate high-focus scanning. The pupils should feel like they are locked onto a target.
- **Jaw Tension:** We need visible clenching. The jawline should feel sharper, pulling the corners of the mouth into a tighter, straighter line.
- **Mouth State:** The lips should be slightly parted, as if the person is breathing through their mouth in a state of hyper-arousal.
- **Brow:** The brow should be slightly more active—not just raised in surprise, but perhaps with the inner corners pulled slightly together to show the cognitive load of a crisis.

### The EXHAUSTED Pole (-1.0)
This currently reads as "sleepy" or "uninterested." To capture the "capitulation" of a trader who has watched a position bleed for weeks:
- **Heaviness:** The eyelids need to be significantly heavier, drooping at least halfway. We need to see more "baggage" or sagging in the lower lids to show the physical toll.
- **Slackness:** The jaw should be completely surrendered to gravity. The mouth should be slightly open, but unlike the "Wired" state, it should feel limp—lips soft and corners drooping significantly.
- **The Stare:** The "thousand-yard stare" is missing. The eyes should feel like they've lost the ability to focus on the foreground, perhaps feeling slightly "glassy" or distant.

### The Middle Range (±0.4)
The distinction between "slightly tired" and "slightly wired" is currently too fine to be useful. 
- To make **Slightly Wired (+0.4)** pop, add a tiny flare to the nostrils and a slight lift of the inner brow.
- To make **Slightly Tired (-0.4)** pop, the primary cue should be a softening of the overall facial mask—the "resting" tension should drain out, particularly around the eyes.

## SET 2 — THE QUADRANTS (FATIGUE × ALARM)

### Euphoric + Exhausted (The "Hollow Victory")
This currently reads as "mildly drunk" or "pleasantly dazed." It lacks the "spent" quality. The smile is too "active." To make this feel like "blissful exhaustion," the smile should be "lazy"—the eyes should be nearly closed while the mouth holds a faint, effortless upturn. It should feel like someone who has won but is too tired to celebrate.

### Euphoric + Wired (The "Manic High")
This is the most successful composition. The combination of wide eyes and a smile creates a "disturbed" or "unhinged" energy that perfectly captures a market bubble. It feels like someone who can't look away from a parabolic move. We can push this even further by making the smile "tighter"—less "happy" and more "manic."

### Alarmed + Exhausted (The "Capitulation")
This currently feels "confused." The "Alarm" (raised brows) is fighting the "Exhaustion" (heavy eyes) in a way that just cancels out. To make this work, the alarm must be "defeated." The eyebrows should stay high (fear), but the eyes themselves should be drooping (exhausted). It should look like someone who is terrified of what's happening but is physically unable to stay awake or respond.

### Alarmed + Wired (The "Pure Panic")
This reads as "Busy" or "Scared," but not quite "Panic." The ingredients are there, but the "Wired" jaw tension needs to be cranked up. The person should look like they are vibrating. Adding a slight "asymmetry" to the mouth or brow could help convey the chaotic nature of a panic state.
