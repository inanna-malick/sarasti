## 3. Real-Time Dynamics (The Morph)

If these were morphing in real-time, the transition that would catch my eye fastest is **Neutral to Alarm**.
If a face goes from a flat stare to those wide "Alarm" eyes in half a second, I don't need to read a headline. I know the volatility just spiked. It’s an **instant** read. I’d react before I even thought about why.

Second fastest: **Aggression to Fatigue**. Seeing a "tough" face start to sag and get "puffy" tells me the momentum is dying. That’s a "take profit" signal that takes about **1 second** to register.

## 4. What's MISSING?

**Deception.** 
The market is a liar. Every big move starts with a head-fake. I don't see a "smirk" or a "shifty" look here. I need a face that looks like it's trying to hide a massive order. Where’s the "Iceberg Order" face? Where’s the "Fake-out" expression? 
Right now, these faces are too "honest." If they just show the current state, they're just fancy lag indicators. I need to see *intent*.

---

## 5. Brutal Honesty: Gimmick or Tool?

Is it a gimmick? **Partially.** You’re never going to replace the raw data for the final execution.

But would I use it? **Yeah, as a peripheral monitor.**
If I’ve got 50 instruments to track, I can’t watch 50 charts. But I can scan a wall of 50 faces. My brain is hard-wired to detect a "scared" face in a crowd in milliseconds. 
If I see a sea of "Neutral" faces and one guy in the corner starts looking "Aggressive" and "Wired," I’m clicking on that instrument immediately. 

It’s not a dashboard; it’s a **Pre-Attentive Alert System**. 

**Verdict:** Keep the "Alarm" and "Aggression" axes. Trash the "Puffy/Sharp" stuff unless you can map it better to liquidity. And for God's sake, add a "Smirk" for when the HFTs are messing with us.
