## 1. Clear Signals (Instant Reads)
A child would read these instantly without any hesitation:
*   **`full_puffy-soyboi-young-euphoric.png`**: Pure happiness. The "round" face helps children trust this person.
*   **`aggression_1.5.png`**: Pure anger. The furrowed brow is a universal "stop" sign for kids.
*   **`alarm_2.0.png`**: Pure fear/surprise. The wide eyes and open mouth are unmistakable.
*   **`fatigue_3.0.png`**: Pure exhaustion. Children know exactly what it feels like when their eyes won't stay open.

## 2. Confusing/Uncanny Faces
These would make a child tilt their head or say "they look weird":
*   **`fatigue_-3.0.png`**: The eyes are too wide and static. It looks "frozen" or like a doll, which triggers the "creepy" response in children.
*   **`shape_sharp-soyboi.png`**: The neck and chin structure look unnatural (uncanny valley). A child might focus on the "bird-like" shape rather than any emotion.
*   **`alarm_-1.5.png`**: This is ambiguous. Is he suspicious? Is he just trying to see something far away? A child would likely hedge: "He looks mean, maybe?"

## 4. Scary Faces (Child would look away)
*   **`fatigue_-3.0.png`**: The "death stare" is very unsettling. It looks like a person who isn't blinking.
*   **`full_sharp-chad-old-alarmed.png`**: The combination of intense fear and an "old" face can be frightening to a child, as they often associate grandpas with safety, not extreme panic.

## 5. "The Same Person"
*   **`neutral.png`** and **`aggression_0.5.png`** look the most like the same person captured just seconds apart. The identity is perfectly preserved, only the muscle tension changed.
*   Conversely, **`shape_puffy-chad.png`** and **`shape_sharp-soyboi.png`** look like two completely different people (perhaps brothers, but distinct individuals).
