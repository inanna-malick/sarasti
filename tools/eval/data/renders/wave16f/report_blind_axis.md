# Blind Facial Expression Test Report (Wave 16f)

## Neutral Baseline
The neutral baseline shows a bald male character with a standard, expressionless face. The mouth is closed, eyebrows are in a relaxed position, and the gaze is forward. This serves as the reference point for all other axes.

---

## Axis A
**Description:**
- **Positive Pole:** A subtle but clear smile. The mouth corners are upturned, and there is a slight lift in the cheeks. It conveys a sense of friendliness, satisfaction, or mild happiness.
- **Negative Pole:** A slightly open mouth with a "slack" appearance. The eyebrows are marginally raised. It looks like a state of mild surprise, confusion, or disappointment.

**Top 3 Guesses:**
1. Valence (Happiness vs. Sadness/Disappointment)
2. Satisfaction
3. Engagement

**Ratings:**
- **Readability:** 4/5 (The smile is very clear; the negative pole is slightly more ambiguous but distinct).
- **Differentiation:** 4/5 (The poles represent clearly different emotional states).

**Notes:**
The negative pole looks slightly artificial/uncanny due to the slackness of the jaw without much accompanying eye movement.

---

## Axis B
**Description:**
- **Positive Pole:** A very subtle compression of the lips. The face looks stoic or pensive.
- **Negative Pole:** A slightly more open mouth and a seemingly more intense or focused gaze. It could be interpreted as "active listening" or increased alertness.

**Top 3 Guesses:**
1. Arousal / Alertness
2. Intensity / Focus
3. Stoicism vs. Expressiveness

**Ratings:**
- **Readability:** 2/5 (The changes are extremely subtle and hard to label confidently).
- **Differentiation:** 2/5 (The two poles are quite similar).

**Notes:**
The positive pole has a slightly "pinched" look around the mouth that feels a bit stiff.

---

## Axis C
**Description:**
- **Positive Pole:** Pursing of the lips and a slight furrowing of the brow. It conveys skepticism, disapproval, or a "wait, what?" reaction.
- **Negative Pole:** A relaxed, almost content expression, very close to the neutral baseline but perhaps slightly "softer."

**Top 3 Guesses:**
1. Approval vs. Disapproval
2. Skepticism
3. Tension vs. Relaxation

**Ratings:**
- **Readability:** 3/5 (The "disapproval" in the positive pole is clear, but the negative pole is almost indistinguishable from neutral).
- **Differentiation:** 3/5 (One pole is active, the other is baseline-adjacent).

**Notes:**
The lip pursing in the positive pole feels a bit "digital" or blocky in its execution.

---

## Axis D
**Description:**
- **Positive Pole:** A distinct lopsided smirk. One side of the mouth is raised significantly higher than the other. The eyes appear slightly narrowed. It conveys smugness, confidence, or a knowing sarcasm.
- **Negative Pole:** Almost identical to the neutral baseline, perhaps with a slightly flatter affect.

**Top 3 Guesses:**
1. Smugness / Confidence
2. Sarcasm / Irony
3. Asymmetry / Lopsidedness

**Ratings:**
- **Readability:** 4/5 (The smirk is a very recognizable social cue).
- **Differentiation:** 3/5 (Like Axis C, it's mostly a one-sided deviation from neutral).

**Notes:**
The asymmetry is well-rendered but borders on uncanny if the character isn't supposed to be "villainous" or overly cocky.
