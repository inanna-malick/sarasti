## C. What emotion is missing?

*   **ALARM:** Between 0.0 and 0.5, we are missing **"Shock"** (sudden high-arousal surprise); the axis currently moves straight from neutral into a slow-burn "Concern."
*   **FATIGUE:** Between 0.0 and -0.5, we are missing **"Drowsiness"** (unfocused, wandering gaze); the character looks "weary" but still mentally present.
*   **AGGRESSION:** Between 0.0 and 0.5, we are missing **"Contempt"** (asymmetrical lip curl); the snarl is currently too symmetrical and combat-focused from the start.

## D. Cross-Axis Confusion

1.  **alarm_0.5 and aggression_-0.5:** Both faces present as "generalized worry." Without the snarl or the euphoric smile, the brow-pinch is semantically ambiguous between concern and submission.
2.  **fatigue_1.5 and alarm_1.0:** Both read as "anxious focus." The hyper-alertness of the wired state is difficult to distinguish from the high-arousal dread of alarm.
3.  **fatigue_-1.5 and aggression_-1.5:** Both read as "defeat/hopelessness." The combination of open mouth and slack features creates a shared visual language of "giving up."

## E. The Money Question

**Weakest Face:** `fatigue_0.5` (Alert Neutral)

**Problem:** This face is the most ambiguous in the entire set. It intended to signal "active readiness," but in isolation, it often reads as a "thousand-yard stare" or simply a slightly wider-eyed neutral. It lacks the physiological tension required to differentiate it from a character who is simply "present."

**The Fix:** We need to increase the tension in the lower eyelids (ψ25) and introduce a subtle tightening of the neck/jaw muscles. The face should look like it is *expecting* an input, not just *receiving* one.
