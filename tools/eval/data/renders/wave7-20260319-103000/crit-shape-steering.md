## B. Maturity Gradient Notes

*   **-3.0:** ~5 years old. Very round, massive forehead, huge eyes.
*   **-2.0:** ~13 years old. Pre-pubescent, features starting to descend.
*   **-1.0:** ~20 years old. Early adult, baby fat still present.
*   **0.0:** ~32 years old. Baseline adult.
*   **+1.0:** ~50 years old. Gravity begins to take hold; jawline widens.
*   **+2.0:** ~70 years old. Significant vertical compression of features.
*   **+3.0:** 90+ years old. Extreme sagging/gaunt hybrid.

**Observations:** The progression is smooth. "Young" (neoteny) effectively stops at -0.5. "Old" (senescence) starts feeling distinct at +1.0.

## C. Missing Geometric Features of Aging

While the current maturity axis captures "facial height" and "sagging" well, it misses several key β-shape components of human aging:
1.  **Temple Recession:** Real aging involves bone resorption and fat loss at the temples. These renders stay too wide at the top of the head in older versions.
2.  **Lip Thinning:** The lips remain plush even at +3.0. Older faces should show a marked loss of vermillion volume (thinning lips).
3.  **Under-Eye Hollows:** The orbital rim should become more prominent as the infraorbital fat pads migrate or diminish.
4.  **Lateral Brow Descent:** The eyebrows stay too high and horizontal; a "tired" look requires the outer edges of the brow to drop significantly.
5.  **Jowl Breaks:** The jawline widens, but it lacks the specific "jowl" notch (pre-jowl sulcus) where skin folds over the bone.

## D. Independence Test

**Dominance vs. Maturity:**
*   **Dominance** modifies the **X-axis (width)** and bone density (jaw, brow, neck thickness).
*   **Maturity** modifies the **Y-axis (gravity)** and feature proportions (eye size, vertical compression).

They are highly independent and reinforce each other. A "Dominant Mature" face looks like a powerful but aging patriarch; a "Dominant Young" face looks like an athlete. They do not fight or create uncanny artifacts when combined at ±1.5.

## E. The Third Axis: "Sharpness" (Leanness vs. Adiposity)

If we add a third structural dimension, it should be **Sharpness**.

*   **Pole 1: Emaciated/Angular (-3.0).** Sunken cheeks, razor-sharp jawline, visible zygomatic arches, "hungry" look. 
    *   *Trader Signal:* **High Volatility / Efficiency.** This instrument is lean, fast, but potentially desperate.
*   **Pole 2: Puffy/Soft (+3.0).** High facial fat, hidden bone structure, round cheeks, double chin, "bloated" look.
    *   *Trader Signal:* **Complacency / Stagnation.** This instrument is stable but over-leveraged and slow-moving.

This would allow us to differentiate a "Healthy Old Chad" (Mature + Dominant + Sharp) from a "Bloated Old Chad" (Mature + Dominant + Puffy).
