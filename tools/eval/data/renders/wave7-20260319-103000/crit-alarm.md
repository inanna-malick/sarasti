# Art Director Review: ALARM Axis (Wave 7)
**Timestamp:** 2026-03-19T10:30Z
**Date:** 2026-03-19
**Axis:** Alarmed (Positive) ↔ Euphoric (Negative)
**Increments:** 0.5

## Executive Summary
Wave 7 successfully shifts the ALARM axis from a "Surprise ↔ Smugness" profile to a "Dread ↔ Elation" profile. the addition of brow pinching and mouth opening significantly improves the emotional readability at the operational dashboard range (0.5 to 1.5).

---

## 1. Alarmed Pole: FEAR/DREAD Analysis
**Goal:** Move away from "shocked surprise" towards "worried threat-response."

*   **0.5 (Concern):** Brows are already showing the ψ3 pinch. It looks like the character has spotted something "not quite right." Read: Vigilant/Concerned.
*   **1.0 (Alarm):** Brow furrow is prominent. Mouth is slightly agape. The ψ16 focus makes the gaze feel "locked" on a threat. Read: Alarmed/Anxious.
*   **1.5 (Dread):** The brow is tightly knotted. The chin retraction (ψ26) creates a "pulling back" sensation, as if recoiling from a threat. Read: Fear/Dread.

**Verdict:** Successful. The brow pinch is the MVP here. It no longer looks like the character is just surprised to see you; they look genuinely threatened.

---

## 2. Euphoric Pole: ELATION/JOY Analysis
**Goal:** Move away from "smug satisfaction" towards "expansive elation."

*   **-0.5 (Pleasure):** A soft, healthy-looking smile. The ψ19 cheeks add a youthful "glow." Read: Content.
*   **-1.0 (Joy):** Mouth is opening naturally. The reduction in ψ7 (squint) allows the eyes to remain "alive" and communicative rather than looking "cunning." Read: Happy.
*   **-1.5 (Elation):** Expansive jaw and open mouth create a "triumphant" look. The confident chin (ψ26) supports the high-arousal positive state. Read: Elated/Triumphant.

**Verdict:** Successful. The open mouth (ψ0=1.5) and expansive jaw (ψ15=-0.5) are transformative. It feels like a release of energy rather than a suppressed smirk.

---

## 3. Gradient Quality & Progression
*   **0.0 to 0.5:** Highly distinct. The shift from neutral to "pinched brow" is the most immediate visual cue.
*   **0.5 to 1.5:** Linear and smooth. Each 0.5 step adds a noticeable layer of intensity to both the brow and the mouth aperture.
*   **Plateau:** The expression does not plateau; it continues to intensify to 3.0. However, the "sweet spot" for high-fidelity rendering is 0.0 to 1.8.

---

## 4. Areas for Improvement
*   **Alarmed:** The mouth opening at 1.0-1.5 is a bit "round." A slightly more horizontal "stretch" of the mouth corners (fear-grin) might enhance the "terror" aspect, though the current "O" shape works well for "dread."
*   **Euphoric:** At -1.5, the upper lip could be slightly more arched to emphasize the "smile" shape within the open mouth.

---

## 5. Artifact Thresholds
*   **Alarmed @ 2.5+:** The chin retraction starts to look "collapsed" into the neck, and the brow pinch creates extreme geometry folds that might flicker in lower LODs.
*   **Euphoric @ -2.5+:** The cheeks (ψ19) are so aggressive they begin to "swallow" the lower eyelid, making the character look squinty again, but this time due to volume displacement rather than muscle contraction.

## Final Approval
**APPROVED for Wave 7.** The changes to brow pinching and mouth expansion have solved the primary readability issues from Wave 6.
