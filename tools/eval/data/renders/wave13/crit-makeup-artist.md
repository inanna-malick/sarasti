## Top 5 Best Marriages (Expression + Skin Color)
1. **`cross_wired-aggressive.png`**: The intense flush perfectly communicates a volatile, "about to blow" state.
2. **`cross_euphoric-yielding.png`**: The soft, warm glow is a masterclass in communicating peaceful bliss.
3. **`cross_exhausted-yielding.png`**: The combination of a resigned look and sallow skin perfectly captures total defeat.
4. **`exhausted_1.0.png`**: Very effective use of desaturation to show a complete drain of vitality.
5. **`full_calm.png`**: A healthy, warm "glow" that makes the face feel alive and stable.

## Top 5 Worst Mismatches
1. **`alarmed_1.0.png`**: Major physiological error. Fear should be pale, not flushed red.
2. **`full_crisis.png`**: Again, too red. In a financial "crisis," the face should look like the blood has left it.
3. **`euphoric_1.0.png`**: The color is a bit too orange/bronzed, like a fake tan, rather than a natural blood-flow glow.
4. **`smirk_1.0.png`**: The skin color is too subtle to help communicate the state at small sizes.
5. **`yielding_1.0.png`**: Missed opportunity to use skin tone to support the submissive/open expression.

## Specific Recommendations
- **Alarmed & Crisis States**: These need a "vasoconstriction" effect. Shift the skin tones toward a cooler (slightly blue/grey) and paler palette. Reduce saturation significantly to show shock.
- **Euphoric State**: Move away from the orange/bronze "tan" look. Instead, use a vibrant pinkish-red glow concentrated in the "blush zones" (cheeks, forehead, chin) to simulate high oxygenation and joy.
- **Thumbnail Optimization (64x64)**: For subtle expressions like `smirk` and `yielding`, the skin color needs to work harder. 
    - Give `smirk` a slightly "unbalanced" or localized flush to suggest hidden intent.
    - Give `yielding` a slightly warmer, softer tone to differentiate it from `neutral` at a distance.
- **Aggressive State**: The flush is good, but adding a slight specular "sheen" or "sweat" would heighten the realism of a high-arousal aggressive state.
