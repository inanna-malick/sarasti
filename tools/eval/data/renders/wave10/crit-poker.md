## Final Report

1. **Which faces look like they're HIDING something? What specifically makes you think that?**
   `cross_smirk-alarmed.png` and `full_smirk-alarmed-aggressive.png` are the biggest liars. In both, the mouth is doing the "I'm confident" dance with a smirk, but the eyes are wide, showing too much white (sclera). That's a classic fear response. When the top of the face is terrified and the bottom is gloating, he's bluffing his head off.

2. **Which faces look the most HONEST/transparent?**
   `neutral.png` is the most honest because it doesn't try to sell me a story. Beyond that, `alarm_-1.5.png` and `fatigue_-2.0.png` are brutally honest—you can't fake that level of pure panic or "please kill me" exhaustion.

3. **Are there any faces that look like the same person making different expressions? Which ones break the illusion of being the same person?**
   The base model is clearly the same guy throughout, but the `shape_` series breaks the illusion. `shape_puffy-smirk.png` looks like the guy's softer, friendlier brother, while `shape_sharp-smirk.png` looks like the guy after six months of intense gym work and a divorce. The `soyboi` and `chad` variants feel like different characters entirely.

4. **If these faces were arranged on a wall and one suddenly changed expression, which transitions would you notice fastest? Which would you miss?**
   I'd catch `neutral.png` to `alarm_1.5.png` instantly—it's like a fire alarm going off. I'd almost certainly miss a transition between `sweetspot_fatigue_0.8.png` and `sweetspot_fatigue_1.2.png`; that's a shift in internal focus that doesn't leak much to the surface.

5. **Are there any faces where the mouth says one thing and the eyes say another? Describe the contradiction.**
   Look at `cross_smirk-alarmed.png`. The mouth is pulled up into a smug "I've got you" smirk, but the eyes are wide and darting, like he's looking for the exit. It's the face of a man who just shoved all-in with 7-high and realized his opponent is an unbluffable calling station.
