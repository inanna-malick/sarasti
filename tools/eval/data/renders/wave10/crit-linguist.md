# Cognitive Linguistic Analysis of Rendered Emotional Expressions (Wave 10)

## Analysis of Individual Faces

### 1. Neutral Baseline
**`neutral.png`**
- **American English Label:** Neutral / Stoic
- **Japanese Label:** 真面目 (Majime - serious/earnest) / 空気を読む (Kuuki o yomu - baseline readiness)
- **Physical Description:** Brows level, mouth closed and horizontal, eyes centered with standard aperture, head at zero angle, skin tone even.

### 2. Aggression Series
**`aggression_-1.5.png` to `aggression_1.5.png`**
- **American English Label:** Varying from "Slightly Annoyed" (-0.5) to "Furious" (1.5)
- **Japanese Label:** 不機嫌 (Fukigen - bad mood) to 怒り (Ikari - anger) / 威圧感 (Iyatsukan - intimidation)
- **Physical Description:** Graduated lowering and furrowing of the brow (corrugator supercilii activity), increasing mouth tension with lower lip pulling down, narrowing of eye aperture (squint), slight forward head tilt.

### 3. Alarm Series
**`alarm_-1.5.png` to `alarm_1.5.png`**
- **American English Label:** Varying from "Slightly Surprised" to "Terrified/Panicked"
- **Japanese Label:** 驚き (Odoroki - surprise) / 困惑 (Konwaku - bewilderment/distress)
- **Physical Description:** Graduated elevation of the brows (frontalis activity), increasing eye aperture (widening), mouth opening into an oval shape (jaw drop), slight backward head tilt.

### 4. Fatigue Series
**`fatigue_-2.0.png` to `fatigue_2.0.png`**
- **American English Label:** Varying from "Relaxed" to "Exhausted/Dead-tired"
- **Japanese Label:** 我慢 (Gaman - enduring/persevering through exhaustion) / 疲れ (Tsukare - fatigue)
- **Physical Description:** Graduated drooping of the upper eyelids (ptosis), slight mouth laxity (jaw relaxation), neutral to slightly lowered brow, eyes losing focus/directness.

### 5. Smirk Series
**`smirk_0.3.png` to `smirk_2.5.png`**
- **American English Label:** Varying from "Amused" to "Smug/Arrogant"
- **Japanese Label:** 苦笑い (Nigawarai - bitter smile/forced amusement) / 余裕 (Yoyuu - composure/confidence)
- **Physical Description:** Asymmetric elevation of one corner of the mouth (buccinator/risorius activity), slight narrowing of eyes on the same side, brow remaining relatively neutral.

### 8. The Sweetspot and Base Series
(Summary of ranges)
- **Aggression (0.5 to 1.5)**: Graduated furrowing of the brow and mouth tension.
- **Alarm (0.5 to 1.5)**: Graduated brow elevation and mouth aperture expansion.
- **Fatigue (0.5 to 2.0)**: Graduated upper eyelid ptosis and jaw laxity.
- **Smirk (0.3 to 2.5)**: Graduated unilateral zygomaticus/risorius activation.
- **Sweetspots (0.8, 1.2)**: Idealized prototypes for each category.
- **Negative offsets (-0.5 to -2.0)**: Subtle deviations toward relaxation or paradoxical contraction.

## Synthesis & Evaluation

### 1. UNIVERSAL Readability
- **`alarm_1.5.png`**: High-arousal alarm (widened eyes, open mouth) is near-universally recognized across cultures as fear or extreme surprise.
- **`aggression_1.5.png`**: The "anger" prototype (lowered/furrowed brow) is highly consistent globally.
- **`neutral.png`**: While its *meaning* (earnest vs. cold) varies, the physical configuration is recognized as a baseline.

### 2. CULTURALLY AMBIGUOUS Faces
- **`smirk_2.5.png`**: In American contexts, this reads as "I'm better than you" (arrogance). In Japanese contexts, it can read as 苦笑い (Nigawarai) — a way to politely mask discomfort or acknowledge a difficult/ironic situation where one has to "grin and bear it."
- **`fatigue_1.5.png`**: Americans read this as "Sleepy/Unproductive." Japanese observers may see it as 我慢 (Gaman) — the face of someone working through their limit, which is culturally valued.
- **`cross_euphoric-yielding.png`**: Can read as "Friendly Submissiveness" (US) vs. 謙虚 (Kenkyo - modesty/humility) (JP).

### 3. VISUAL SIMILARITY CLUSTERS
I identify **5 natural clusters** based on visual configuration:
1.  **Cluster A: Vertical Expansion (Alarm/Surprise)**: Raised brows, widened eyes, vertical mouth opening.
2.  **Cluster B: Vertical Contraction (Aggression/Anger)**: Lowered brows, narrowed eyes, horizontal mouth tension.
3.  **Cluster C: Gravity-Dominant (Fatigue/Exhaustion)**: Drooping lids, lax jaw, overall lack of muscle tension.
4.  **Cluster D: Asymmetric Tension (Smirks/Irony)**: Unilateral mouth elevation, often with divergent eye/brow states.
5.  **Cluster E: Soft/Rounded States (Moderate/Positive)**: Symmetrical smiling, relaxed eye aperture, lack of extreme contraction.

### 4. EMOTION GAPS
The current vocabulary lacks:
- **Disgust/Contempt**: No nose wrinkling or upper lip elevation (levator labii superioris alaeque nasi activity).
- **Sadness/Grief**: The "inner brow raise" (obliquity) that signals genuine distress is missing.
- **Thought/Contemplation**: Asymmetric brow furrowing or lip biting associated with problem-solving.
- **Contempt (Bilateral)**: The specific "lip corner tightening" (contempt) distinct from a smile or smirk.

### 5. BEST ICON CANDIDATES
- **`alarm_1.2.png`**: Extremely readable at low resolution; the silhouette is distinct.
- **`aggression_1.2.png`**: The brow furrow creates a strong T-shape visible from a distance.
- **`smirk_1.5.png`**: The asymmetry is a strong visual "hook" that signals irony or attitude.
- **`fatigue_2.0.png`**: The eyelid droop is unmistakable as a signal of low energy.
