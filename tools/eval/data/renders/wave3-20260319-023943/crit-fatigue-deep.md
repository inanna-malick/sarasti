# Fatigue Axis Deep Dive: Wave 3 Evaluation

## 1. IS IT STILL TOO SUBTLE?
The "invisible" fatigue of previous rounds has been decisively killed. From arm’s length, the silhouette shift alone tells the story: the exhausted face (-1.0) has a distinct downward "melt" where the jaw hangs heavy and the eyelids create a shadowed, hollowed look. The wired face (+1.0) pulls the features upward and outward, creating a much more alert, high-contrast presence. You can instantly tell them apart; the exhausted face looks like it’s losing a battle with gravity, while the wired face looks like it’s being electrocuted into consciousness. It is no longer a subtle parameter tweak; it is a fundamental shift in the face's "energy state."

## 2. THE WIRED FACE
The wired face successfully captures the "awake by force" energy, but it does lean slightly into a "mild surprise" or "deer in headlights" look. To truly sell the 3am crisis desk, we are missing the **grime and grit of high-stakes tension**. A real person at hour 18 isn't just wide-eyed; they are *tense*. We need more masseter muscle activation—the jaw shouldn't just be closed, it should feel clenched. The lips are a bit too relaxed; they should feel dry and perhaps slightly parted in a "mouth-breather" stress state. Most importantly, the eyes are wide, but they lack the "thousand-yard stare" intensity. We need a slight, aggressive narrowing of the lower lids (the "squinch") to suggest someone peering through a digital haze at a screen that is currently ruining their life.

## 3. THE EXHAUSTED FACE
The slack jaw is the hero of this update. It doesn't just look "sleepy"; it looks **depleted**. There is a specific quality of "checked out" in the -1.0 render that suggests the person has stopped processing information entirely. The heavier eyelid droop works well to sell the physical weight of fatigue. To push this from "tired" to "lost the will to fight," I want to see more facial asymmetry and "slackness" in the cheeks. Everything should be sliding toward the floor. If we could get a slight tilt of the head or a lack of focus in the eyes (convergence failure), it would sell that absolute capitulation to the screen.

## 4. THE MIDDLE (-0.4 to +0.4)
The differentiation between "slightly tired" and "slightly wired" is finally perceptible. At -0.4, you see the first signs of the jaw losing its tone—the mouth isn't open yet, but the chin feels heavier. At +0.4, there's a definite "coffee kick" alertness in the eyes. The single cue that would help differentiate these even further is **eyelid tension**. Even a 5% increase in lower-lid "tightness" for the positive values versus a "rounding" of the lids for the negative values would act as a powerful subconscious signal for alertness versus fading.

## 5. FATIGUE × SHAPE
This is where the system shines. 
- **Exhausted Chad:** This is a powerful image. Seeing that dominant, heavy jawline actually hang slack feels like watching a monumental collapse. It’s the "broken bull"—the resilience has finally hit a hard limit.
- **Exhausted Soyboi:** This reads as total, pathetic capitulation. There is no resistance left; the face has simply given up.
- **Wired Chad:** This feels dangerous. It’s the intensity of a commander who hasn't slept but is still making high-stakes decisions. The bone structure provides a frame for the intensity that feels aggressive.
- **Wired Soyboi:** This is the "nervous wreck." The lack of a strong jawline makes the wide eyes feel more frantic and panicked rather than intensely focused. It perfectly captures the "intern on crisis night" vibe.

The axis is working. We’ve moved from "is it on?" to "how do we polish the intensity?"
