# Comparative Review: Wave 2 vs Wave 3 Renders

## ALARM AXIS — wave 2 vs wave 3: (Euphoric, Neutral, Alarmed)

**Euphoric (alarm_-1.0):**
1. **Improvement:** The "Duchenne" crinkle in the eyes and the subtle nose crinkle in Wave 3 make the smile feel significantly more genuine and warm. Wave 2 looked slightly "polite" or robotic by comparison.
2. **Worse:** No major regressions; the nose crinkle is subtle enough that it doesn't create uncanny bunching.
3. **Missing:** Crow's feet texture or subtle skin bunching around the eyes would push this from "modeled" to "felt."

**Alarmed (alarm_1.0):**
1. **Improvement:** The added jaw tension in Wave 3 transforms the face from "surprised" to "scared." The gasp feels more visceral because the mouth corners are pulled tighter.
2. **Worse:** The dark interior of the mouth is very prominent now; without teeth or a tongue, it looks like a black void.
3. **Missing:** Vertical forehead tension (corrugator) or "stress lines" would amplify the panic.

## FATIGUE AXIS — wave 2 vs wave 3: (Exhausted, Neutral, Wired)

**Exhausted (fatigue_-1.0):**
1. **Improvement:** Moving from a pursed mouth (Wave 2) to a slack-open jaw (Wave 3) was the right move. Combined with the heavier eyelid droop, the face finally reads as "surrendered" and truly depleted.
2. **Worse:** The "slack" mouth occasionally looks a bit like a "duh" face in certain lighting; needs more weight in the lower lip.
3. **Missing:** "Glassy" eye reflection or slight redness in the sclera to communicate eye strain.

**Wired (fatigue_1.0):**
1. **Improvement:** Replacing the "curious" raised brow with a tense pinch was a massive upgrade. It now communicates hyper-focus and stress rather than just alertness.
2. **Worse:** The lip parting can look a bit "fish-mouthed" without the corresponding tension in the cheeks.
3. **Missing:** Pupil dilation. A "wired" person should have pin-prick or wide pupils depending on the stress type; the current neutral pupils break the illusion.

## QUADRANTS — wave 2 vs wave 3:

**Panic (alarmed-wired):**
The combination of the tense brow pinch and the high-intensity gasp creates a very readable "flash crash" panic. It's much more aggressive than Wave 2's wide-eyed surprise.

**Hollow Victory (euphoric-exhausted):**
This is one of the most successful blends. The genuine eye crinkle from Wave 3 "Euphoric" mixed with the slack-jawed exhaustion creates a perfect "we survived but at what cost" expression.

**Manic (euphoric-wired):**
The contrast between the "happy" eyes and the "stressed" brow pinch is fantastic. It looks genuinely unhinged and captures the "market bubble" energy perfectly.

**Capitulation (alarmed-exhausted):**
The wide, startled eyes mixed with the defeated, slack mouth create a haunting "deer in headlights" look. It communicates a state of being overwhelmed and unable to act.

## What's Left

1. **The "Black Hole" Mouth:** Every open-mouthed expression (Alarmed, Exhausted, Panic) currently terminates in a flat black void. Adding even a low-poly set of teeth and a tongue would ground the face in 3D space.
2. **Neck and Trapezius Tension:** For Wired and Alarmed states, the face is doing all the work. Adding "stringy" neck muscle tension (platysma/SCM) would communicate that the whole body is reacting to the crisis.
3. **Pupil Response:** We need to tie pupil dilation to the Fatigue axis. "Wired" needs dilated pupils to look truly "on edge," while "Exhausted" needs a flatter, more vacant look.
4. **Micro-Expression Textures:** The geometry changes are great, but they need support from normal/bump maps. A brow pinch should create vertical furrows; a Duchenne smile should create crow's feet.
5. **Lip Weight:** In the slack-open "Exhausted" states, the lower lip feels a bit too rigid. It needs to look like gravity is actually pulling it down.
