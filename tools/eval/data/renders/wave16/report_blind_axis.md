# Perception Study: Blind Axis Analysis (Wave 16)

## Neutral Baseline
The neutral baseline shows a calm, expressionless face with relaxed features. The eyes are open normally, brows are level, and the mouth is closed in a straight line.

---

## Axis A
### a) Description
- **Positive:** Mouth is slightly open with the upper lip lifted; brows are slightly raised, and eyes appear more alert/wide.
- **Negative:** Mouth is firmly closed with corners slightly down; brows are slightly lowered/furrowed, and eyes are slightly narrowed or hooded.
### b) Top 3 Guesses
1. **Alertness/Interest ↔ Boredom/Displeasure** (Confidence: High)
2. **Surprise ↔ Disgust** (Confidence: Medium)
3. **High Arousal ↔ Low Arousal** (Confidence: Medium)
### c) Readability Score: 3
Noticeable change in the mouth and brows, but the specific emotion is a bit ambiguous compared to others.
### d) What's Missing
More intense "eye-widening" for the positive pole and more pronounced nose-wrinkling for the negative pole would help distinguish it from simple alertness.

---

## Axis B
### a) Description
- **Positive:** Eyes are very narrow (squinting); brows are lowered and pulled together; mouth is thin and pressed.
- **Negative:** Eyes are wide open showing more iris; brows are raised; mouth is slightly open.
### b) Top 3 Guesses
1. **Concentration/Skepticism ↔ Surprise/Shock** (Confidence: High)
2. **Anger ↔ Fear** (Confidence: Medium)
3. **Suspicion ↔ Amazement** (Confidence: Medium)
### c) Readability Score: 4
The contrast between the narrow, focused eyes and the wide, shocked eyes is very clear.
### d) What's Missing
For the positive pole, a slight jaw clench or more tension in the lower eyelids would enhance the "concentration/anger" feel. For the negative pole, a lower jaw drop would make "surprise" unmistakable.

---

## Axis C
### a) Description
- **Positive:** Inner corners of the brows are slanted upwards; mouth is slightly compressed; eyes have a "pleading" or "sad" look.
- **Negative:** Inner corners of the brows are slanted downwards; eyes are more "determined" or "angry"; mouth is slightly open.
### b) Top 3 Guesses
1. **Sadness ↔ Anger** (Confidence: Very High)
2. **Distress ↔ Determination** (Confidence: High)
3. **Vulnerability ↔ Aggression** (Confidence: Medium)
### c) Readability Score: 5
The brow slant is the "classic" indicator for these two emotions, making the poles instantly recognizable.
### d) What's Missing
Adding "chin boss" (wrinkling of the chin) and more pronounced downturned mouth corners would perfect the sad pole. Adding flared nostrils would perfect the angry pole.

---

## Axis D
### a) Description
- **Positive:** Mouth corners are subtly turned up; eyes appear "softer" with a hint of warmth.
- **Negative:** Mouth corners are subtly turned down; eyes appear slightly "colder" or more vacant.
### b) Top 3 Guesses
1. **Happy ↔ Sad** (Confidence: High)
2. **Positive Valence ↔ Negative Valence** (Confidence: High)
3. **Friendly ↔ Unfriendly** (Confidence: Medium)
### c) Readability Score: 4
While subtle, the change in the mouth corners is a very strong social cue that is easy to pick up.
### d) What's Missing
"Crow's feet" (wrinkles at the outer eye corners) for the positive pole would make it a true Duchenne smile. More pronounced "lip-corner depressor" action for the negative pole would make the sadness clearer.

---

## Comparison Analysis
- **Similarities:** Axis C (Negative) and Axis B (Positive) both lean towards "Anger/Aggression," but use different mechanisms (brow slant vs. eye squinting). Axis C (Positive) and Axis D (Negative) both lean towards "Sadness," but Axis C uses the brows while Axis D uses the mouth.
- **Redundancy:** There is no major redundancy, as each axis seems to isolate a different primary facial muscle group (Mouth/Alertness, Eye Opening, Brow Slant, Mouth Corners).
- **Confusion:** The negative pole of Axis A is the most ambiguous and could be mistaken for a mild version of several other negative states.
