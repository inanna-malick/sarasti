# Axis Z Character Design Review Report

## Image Descriptions

- **baseline.png**: The neutral baseline face is a middle-aged male with average facial width and tissue volume.
- **axis_Z_pos_front.png**: The face becomes noticeably narrower and more elongated. The jawline is sharper, and the cheeks appear leaner or slightly recessed compared to the baseline.
- **axis_Z_neg_front.png**: The face becomes broader and rounder. There is an increase in soft tissue volume, particularly in the buccal area (mid-to-lower cheeks). The jawline is softer and less defined.
- **axis_Z_pos_left34.png**: In 3/4 view, the positive pole reveals a more defined bone structure. The cheekbones are more prominent due to the reduced volume below them, and the jawline is very crisp.
- **axis_Z_neg_left34.png**: In 3/4 view, the negative pole shows a much fuller, rounder profile. The transitions from the cheeks to the jaw are smooth and fleshy, with an overall softer appearance.
- **combo_ZX_pos_front.png**: Combining Axis Z (+) with another positive axis results in an even narrower, leaner, and more elongated face.
- **combo_ZX_neg_front.png**: Combining Axis Z (-) with another negative axis results in a very wide and fleshy face with significantly increased cheek volume.
- **full_pos_front.png**: With all three axes positive, the face is extremely lean and gaunt. The eye sockets appear deeper, and the face looks significantly more angular and aged.
- **full_neg_front.png**: With all three axes negative, the face is very broad and round, showing substantial soft tissue padding throughout.

## Quantitative & Qualitative Evaluation

1. **READABILITY (1-5):** 5
   - The shape change is very immediately apparent. The narrow vs. wide/round distinction is unmistakable.

2. **POLE DIFFERENCE (1-5):** 5
   - The positive and negative poles are extremely distinct from each other.

3. **NATURALNESS (1-5):** 4
   - The faces look like valid human variations (lean vs. fleshy). The extremes in the "full" images are very dramatic but still within the realm of realistic character archetypes.

4. **THUMBNAIL READABILITY (1-5):** 5
   - The silhouette change is significant. At 48x48 pixels, the difference in facial width and cheek volume would be clearly visible.

5. **What SPECIFIC facial features change most?**
   - Jaw width
   - Cheek volume (buccal fat area)
   - Overall face width and elongation
   - Neck width

6. **TOP 3 GUESSES for what this axis represents:**
   1. Facial Adiposity / Soft Tissue Volume
   2. Narrowness vs. Broadness
   3. Sharpness vs. Softness

7. **Does the positive pole look "bony/lean/gaunt" and the negative pole look "soft/padded/round"? Rate how strongly (1-5):** 5
   - This describes the axis perfectly. The positive pole is consistently sharper and leaner, while the negative pole is softer and more padded.
