# Facial Axis Disentanglement Report: Axis X (Jaw) vs Axis Z (Sharp/Puffy)

## 1. VISUAL OVERLAP
**Score: 2 / 5**
Axis X and Axis Z are perceptually distinct. Axis X primarily governs the horizontal expansion of the lower face (mandible width), whereas Axis Z governs the fullness and definition of soft tissue (cheeks, temples, and facial "leaness"). While both can influence the perception of "dominance" or "softness," they operate on different anatomical layers (skeletal width vs. subcutaneous volume).

## 2. Axis X Unique Features
- **Mandible Width:** Specifically the distance between the gonial angles.
- **Chin Breadth:** The horizontal span of the chin.
- **Lower Face Aspect Ratio:** Changes the face from a "V" or "heart" shape to a "square" or "rectangular" shape.

## 3. Axis Z Unique Features
- **Cheek Fullness (Malar Volume):** Affects the roundness of the midface.
- **Temporal/Infraorbital Definition:** "Sharp" creates hollows under the eyes and in the temples; "Puffy" fills them.
- **Facial "Sharpness":** Definition of the nose bridge and orbital rims.
- **Perceived Body Fat:** "Puffy" looks like higher BMI; "Sharp" looks like lower BMI.

## 4. Positive Poles (Chad vs. Sharp)
**Could you tell them apart?** Yes.
**Why?** Chad (X+) is defined by the massive width of the jawline, creating a very wide, square silhouette. Sharp (Z+) is defined by gaunt, chiseled features and hollowed cheeks, but the underlying jaw structure remains closer to the neutral baseline in terms of width. A face can be "Chad" but "Puffy" (wide but soft) or "Sharp" but "Soyboi" (gaunt but narrow).

## 5. Negative Poles (Soyboi vs. Puffy)
**Could you tell them apart?** Yes.
**Why?** Soyboi (X-) is defined by a narrow, tapering jawline that makes the face look triangular or frail. Puffy (Z-) is defined by a lack of definition and increased facial volume, making the face look rounded and "doughy." Soyboi looks like a narrow bone structure; Puffy looks like soft tissue accumulation over an average bone structure.

## 6. DISENTANGLEMENT VERDICT
**YES**
These two axes are perceptually distinct enough to encode different information at a glance. They effectively separate skeletal "dominance/width" from soft-tissue "fitness/fullness."

## 7. Recommendations
None required. The axes are well-disentangled.
