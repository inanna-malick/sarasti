# Facial Expression Axis Perception Study Report (Wave 16b)

## Neutral Baseline
The neutral baseline represents a calm, slightly stern male face. Eyes are open and forward-looking, lips are closed and neutral (no significant upturn or downturn), and brows are in a relaxed, natural position.

---

## Axis A
### a) Description
*   **Positive Pole:** Mouth is significantly open and slightly rounded, as if in a gasp or mid-word. Eyes are noticeably wider than neutral, showing more sclera. Brows are slightly elevated.
*   **Negative Pole:** Mouth is closed with the corners pulled slightly upward into a very subtle, warm smile. Eyes appear more relaxed and slightly narrowed (nasolabial folds are slightly more prominent).

### b) Top 3 Guesses
1.  **Surprised ↔ Pleased/Content** (Confidence: High)
2.  **Shocked ↔ Calm** (Confidence: Medium)
3.  **High Arousal ↔ Positive Valence** (Confidence: Medium)

### c) Readability Score: 4/5
Clear difference in mouth state (open vs. closed) and eye widening. The poles are distinct and suggest a clear emotional shift.

### d) What's Missing?
For the positive pole, even higher brow elevation would sell the "surprise" better. For the negative pole, adding subtle "crow's feet" (eye crinkling) would make the smile feel more genuine and less like a "polite" mask.

---

## Axis B
### a) Description
*   **Positive Pole:** Extremely subtle. The mouth is closed and feels "set" or slightly tight. Brows have a very faint downward pull at the inner corners.
*   **Negative Pole:** Mouth is just barely parted, appearing slack. Eyes look slightly more "empty" or unfocused compared to the positive pole.

### b) Top 3 Guesses
1.  **Focused/Intense ↔ Slack/Dull** (Confidence: Medium)
2.  **Tense ↔ Relaxed** (Confidence: Medium)
3.  **Mild Anger ↔ Mild Fatigue** (Confidence: Low)

### c) Readability Score: 2/5
Very subtle changes. Without a direct comparison to the neutral baseline, it is difficult to identify the intended emotion. The difference between poles is barely perceptible at first glance.

### d) What's Missing?
The positive pole needs more pronounced brow furrowing (corrugator activity) and perhaps a slight narrowing of the eyes. The negative pole needs a more obvious jaw drop or drooping eyelids to signify true slackness or relaxation.

---

## Axis C
### a) Description
*   **Positive Pole:** Mouth is tightly compressed, with the lips forming a thin line. Brows are lowered and slightly brought together. The face looks "strained" or determined.
*   **Negative Pole:** Mouth is slightly open and slack, similar to Axis B neg but perhaps more pronounced. Brows are neutral.

### b) Top 3 Guesses
1.  **Determined/Stubborn ↔ Passive/Bored** (Confidence: Medium)
2.  **Tense ↔ Slack** (Confidence: High)
3.  **Suppressed Anger ↔ Exhaustion** (Confidence: Medium)

### c) Readability Score: 3/5
The tension in the positive pole is clear, but the emotional meaning is ambiguous. It looks like a physical effort or a refusal to speak.

### d) What's Missing?
To lean into "determination," add more brow intensity. To lean into "anger," add nose wrinkling. The negative pole could benefit from a slight head tilt to suggest a lack of engagement.

---

## Axis D
### a) Description
*   **Positive Pole:** Significant asymmetry. One corner of the mouth is pulled upward and slightly outward, while the other remains neutral. One eye is slightly more squinted than the other.
*   **Negative Pole:** Indistinguishable from the neutral baseline. It appears to be a unipolar axis where "Negative" is simply "Neutral."

### b) Top 3 Guesses
1.  **Contemptuous ↔ Neutral** (Confidence: High)
2.  **Smug ↔ Sincere** (Confidence: Medium)
3.  **Sarcastic ↔ Serious** (Confidence: Medium)

### c) Readability Score: 4/5
The asymmetry of the smirk is very easy to spot. The contrast between the asymmetrical positive pole and the symmetrical neutral negative pole is stark.

### d) What's Missing?
To enhance the "contempt" or "sneer," add unilateral nose wrinkling (levator labii superioris alaeque nasi activity) on the same side as the mouth pull. A slight tilt of the head away from the smirk side would also add to the "looking down on someone" feel.

---

## Overall Comparison
*   **Similarities:** Axis B and C both explore mouth tension, but Axis C (compression) is much more readable than Axis B (subtle set).
*   **Distinctiveness:** Axis D is the only axis utilizing asymmetry, which makes it stand out immediately. Axis A is the only one with high kinetic energy (open jaw).
*   **Pole Overlap:** The "Negative" poles of B, C, and D are all very similar (varying degrees of neutral/slack). A's negative pole is the only one that feels like a distinct "opposite" emotion (positive valence) rather than just a lack of the positive pole's intensity.
