# Acting Coach Review: Wave 16b Renders

## Expression: Alarmed
- **FACS DECODE**: AU1+2 (Brow Raise), AU5 (Upper Lid Raise), AU25+26 (Jaw Drop).
- **CONTRADICTION CHECK**: The mouth is too vertical (O-shape); panic usually pulls the corners horizontally (AU20).
- **ACTING NOTE**: "More horizontal stretch in the lip corners and less of a relaxed jaw drop to sharpen the panic."

## Expression: Euphoric
- **FACS DECODE**: AU6 (Cheek Raiser), AU12 (Lip Corner Puller), AU25 (Lips Part).
- **CONTRADICTION CHECK**: Duchenne markers are present, but the smile feels "polite" rather than "bursting."
- **ACTING NOTE**: "Widen the smile to show more upper teeth and push the cheek raisers higher to really sell the joy."

## Expression: Wired
- **FACS DECODE**: AU5 (Upper Lid Raise), AU1+2 (Slight Brow Raise), AU25 (Slight Part).
- **CONTRADICTION CHECK**: Reads more as "surprised" than "wired" (caffeinated/manic intensity).
- **ACTING NOTE**: "Add some jaw tension and slightly lower the inner brows to shift from 'surprised' to 'intense and caffeinated'."

## Expression: Exhausted
- **FACS DECODE**: AU41 (Lid Droop), AU15 (Lip Corner Depressor), AU25+26 (Jaw Hang).
- **CONTRADICTION CHECK**: The brows are too neutral; physical exhaustion often shows "heavy" brows or a slight AU1 effort to keep eyes open.
- **ACTING NOTE**: "Let the jaw hang heavier and allow the head to tilt slightly to one side to communicate total fatigue."

## Expression: Aggressive
- **FACS DECODE**: AU4 (Brow Lowerer), AU10 (Upper Lip Raiser), AU23+24 (Lip Tightener).
- **CONTRADICTION CHECK**: The eyes are too wide (AU5); true aggression usually squints (AU7) to focus on the target.
- **ACTING NOTE**: "Squint the eyes more to focus the rage and lean the chin forward into the camera."

## Expression: Yielding
- **FACS DECODE**: AU1 (Inner Brow Raise), AU15 (Lip Corner Depressor), AU25 (Lips Part).
- **CONTRADICTION CHECK**: Indistinguishable from "sad" or "tired"; yielding usually requires a social/compliant signal.
- **ACTING NOTE**: "Add a submissive tilt to the head and a micro-smile to show you're compliant, not just sad."

## Expression: Smirk
- **FACS DECODE**: Unilateral AU12 (Lip Corner Puller), AU14 (Dimpler).
- **CONTRADICTION CHECK**: The mouth is cynical but the eyes are "dead"; they need the "knowing" spark of AU6.
- **ACTING NOTE**: "Narrow the eye on the side of the smile to give it that 'I know something you don't' spark."
