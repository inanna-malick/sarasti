# Facial Expression Axis Analysis Report (Wave 16e - Blind Test)

## Axis A: Smile-Awe Dimension
- **Description:**
    - **Positive Pole:** A polite, suppressed smile with lips pressed together and slightly upturned corners. Very readable and natural.
    - **Negative Pole:** An open-mouthed expression with a slight "O" shape. Reads as surprise, awe, or the beginning of a vocalization.
- **Top 3 Guesses:**
    1. Valence/Joy vs. Surprise
    2. Mouth Openness (Smile vs. Awe)
    3. Social Engagement (Polite vs. Reactive)
- **Readability Score:** 4/5
- **Consistency:** Excellent. The transition from pressed-lip smile to open-mouth awe is unmistakable from front, left34, and closeup.
- **Improvements:** The negative pole looks slightly like a mid-speech capture. Adding more "eye-widening" to the negative pole would strengthen the "awe/surprise" read.

## Axis B: Intensity-Focus Dimension
- **Description:**
    - **Positive Pole:** Distinct narrowing of the eyes (squinting). Reads as suspicion, intense concentration, or a "glare."
    - **Negative Pole:** Slightly wider eyes and a very slightly parted mouth. Reads as relaxed or mildly alert.
- **Top 3 Guesses:**
    1. Focus/Concentration (Squint vs. Relaxed)
    2. Suspicion / Skepticism
    3. Eye Aperture (Narrow vs. Wide)
- **Readability Score:** 3/5
- **Consistency:** High. The eye-narrowing is the primary feature and is visible across all camera angles.
- **Improvements:** The negative pole is very subtle and nearly indistinguishable from neutral at first glance. Increasing the eye-widening or adding a slight brow raise to the negative pole would improve contrast.

## Axis C: Disapproval-Vacancy Dimension
- **Description:**
    - **Positive Pole:** Strongly pursed and thinned lips with corners pulled down. A classic "sour" or disapproving look.
    - **Negative Pole:** Slightly dropped jaw and slack mouth. Reads as boredom, vacancy, or tiredness.
- **Top 3 Guesses:**
    1. Disapproval / Disgust (Sour vs. Slack)
    2. Lip Tension (Pursed vs. Lax)
    3. Contempt / Rejection
- **Readability Score:** 5/5
- **Consistency:** Exceptional. The pursed lips are extremely distinct even in the profile view.
- **Improvements:** The negative pole is a bit "empty." Adding slightly heavy eyelids to the negative pole could solidify the "bored/vacant" read.

## Axis D: Confidence-Stoicism Dimension
- **Description:**
    - **Positive Pole:** Asymmetrical pull on one corner of the mouth. A classic smirk or "half-smile." Reads as sarcasm, confidence, or playfulness.
    - **Negative Pole:** Extremely close to neutral. Perhaps a very slight tightening of the mouth corners.
- **Top 3 Guesses:**
    1. Smirk / Sarcasm (Asymmetrical vs. Stoic)
    2. Confidence / Smugness
    3. Asymmetry / Unilateral Pull
- **Readability Score:** 4/5
- **Consistency:** Good. The smirk is clearly asymmetrical from the front and closeup. It remains readable from the left34 angle as well.
- **Improvements:** The negative pole is too similar to the neutral baseline. It could be pushed further toward a "frown" or a more intense "stoic" lip-press to create a true bipolar axis.

## General Observations
- **Camera Angles:** The `left34` angle is particularly useful for judging jaw depth and lip protrusion (especially in Axis C).
- **Consistency:** All axes maintain their primary character across the three angles, suggesting high structural integrity in the underlying face model.
- **Subtlety:** Axes B and D have very subtle negative poles that might get lost in low-resolution or high-noise environments.
