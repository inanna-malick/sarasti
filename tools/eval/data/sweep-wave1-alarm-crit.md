# Art Director Critique: ALARM Axis Sweep (Wave 1)

## Overview
This critique evaluates the ALARM axis, ranging from **EUPHORIC (-1.0)** to **ALARMED (+1.0)**. The axis is intended to map market volatility to acute stress responses. Overall, the poles are readable, but the transition curves and mouth integration for the Alarmed state require refinement to reach production quality.

---

## Front Sweep Analysis

### alarm_-1.0_front.png (EUPHORIC Pole)
This face conveys a warm, "fat and happy" satisfaction. The smile is symmetric and wide, which works well for a state of positive deviation. The eye crinkling (ψ7) is the hero here; it makes the expression feel genuine rather than a plastic mask. It perfectly represents the "warm satisfaction" of a market in a steady uptrend.
- **Readability:** High. Distinctly positive.
- **Artifacts:** None. The removal of the asymmetric ψ1 has successfully cleaned up the mouth line.
- **Improvement:** Could benefit from a slightly higher ψ0 (aperture) to pull the corners out and up further, emphasizing the "big win" feel.

### alarm_-0.7_front.png
A clear "contentment" state. It’s a softened version of the pole, looking like a trader who just saw a green candle but isn't celebrating yet. 
- **Readability:** Good. It sits clearly between neutral and euphoric.
- **Comparison:** It’s distinct enough from -1.0, though the mouth shape is very similar.

### alarm_-0.4_front.png
This is a "pleasant" resting state. The eyes have lost their heavy-lidded neutral look and the mouth has just a hint of a curve.
- **Readability:** Subtle. At this level, it might be confused with a slightly "soft" neutral unless compared side-by-side.
- **Improvement:** The transition from 0.0 to -0.4 could be steeper to make this "light positive" state more readable in isolation.

### alarm_0.0_front.png (NEUTRAL)
A solid, vacant baseline. It looks appropriately "bored" or "waiting."
- **Readability:** Perfect as a zero-point. No emotional leakage.

### alarm_0.4_front.png
The face begins to "tighten." The mouth aperture starts to part, and the eyes widen slightly. It reads as "Alert" rather than "Alarmed."
- **Readability:** Good. The slight mouth opening is a strong signal that the resting state has been disturbed.
- **Artifacts:** The lower lip looks a bit flat.

### alarm_0.7_front.png
A state of clear "Concern." The brow lift (ψ8/ψ6) is becoming the dominant feature. 
- **Readability:** High. This is the "What's that noise?" face.
- **Comparison:** Very distinct from 0.4 due to the significant brow movement.

### alarm_1.0_front.png (ALARMED Pole)
This conveys "Acute Shock." The eyes are wide, and the mouth is open. It reads as a "What just happened?" moment, perfectly capturing a flash-crash or sudden volatility spike.
- **Readability:** High, but the mouth integration is weak.
- **Artifacts:** The mouth looks like a dark hole cut into the mesh. It lacks the "pull" of the surrounding cheeks and jaw that usually accompanies a gasp. The jaw feels a bit static despite the opening.
- **Improvement:** We need to blend the mouth opening more into the lower face. The ψ2 component is too "boxy" on its own.

## Recommendations

### General
- **Mouth Integration:** The transition from Neutral to Alarmed (+1.0) needs better muscle integration. The current ψ2 opening looks "additive" rather than "transformative" to the lower face geometry.

### Specific Recipe Changes
*   **For ALARMED (+1.0):**
    *   **Increase ψ0 (Aperture):** Set ψ0 to 0.5 at the +1.0 pole. This will help the mouth opening feel less like a sharp "ψ2-only" hole and more like a natural gasp.
    *   **Adjust ψ6/ψ8 Balance:** Reduce ψ6 slightly (to -1.2) and increase ψ8 (to 2.2). The brow lift feels a bit too "surprised" and not "shocked" enough. Increasing ψ8 will add more forehead creasing.
    *   **Add Jaw Tension:** If we have a jaw-tension component, it should be introduced at +0.7 to +1.0.

*   **For EUPHORIC (-1.0):**
    *   **Increase ψ0 slightly:** Bump ψ0 from 0.75 to 0.9. It will make the smile feel "fuller" and more expressive of a "big win."
    *   **Steepen the curve at -0.4:** The transition from 0.0 to -0.4 is too flat. We need the "satisfaction" to hit earlier in the axis to ensure readability at low deviations.

### Final Verdict
The ALARM axis is 85% there. The Alarmed pole is the priority—fix the "hollow mouth" and it will be production-ready. Euphoria is excellent but could be slightly "louder."
