## Quick Stats
- Distinct visual clusters: 8 (~3.0 bits)
- Mean calm↔crisis contrast: STRONG
- Dynamic range vs w18: WIDER

## Cluster Map
1. **The Wolf of Wall Street (Predatory Vitality)**: High vitality, high aggression, low distress. Features: Intense, focused gaze, firm jaw, confident flush.
   - *Members*: ALI=F (Crisis), CF (Crisis), WTI (Crisis), HO=F (Calm)
   - *w18 Equivalent*: Enhanced Silver-Haired Authority (more aggressive)

2. **The Void (Frozen Panic)**: High distress, low vitality, high alarm. Features: Pale, wide-eyed, slack-jawed, extreme fatigue texture.
   - *Members*: ^VIX (Crisis), WTI (Calm), RB=F (Crisis)
   - *w18 Equivalent*: Frozen Panic

3. **Gilded Zen (Radiant Composure)**: High vitality, low aggression, low distress, low alarm. Features: Warm texture, relaxed features, healthy "composure" glow.
   - *Members*: NG=F (Calm), ^TNX (Crisis), ALI=F (Calm)
   - *w18 Equivalent*: Sleepy Composure (but more positive)

4. **Corporate Beige (Neutral Composure)**: Low signals across all meta-axes. Features: Minimal expression, neutral texture, "gray man" aesthetic.
   - *Members*: XLE (Calm), NG=F (Crisis), CF (Calm)
   - *w18 Equivalent*: Sleepy Composure

5. **Bristling Director (Active Authority)**: High aggression, low distress, moderate vitality. Features: Furrowed brow, intense gaze, controlled energy.
   - *Members*: SPY (Calm)
   - *w18 Equivalent*: Silver-Haired Authority

6. **The Long Afternoon (Sunken Gloom)**: Low vitality, moderate distress, low aggression. Features: Droopy eyes, flat texture, "heavy" or defeated expression.
   - *Members*: GC=F (Crisis), HO=F (Crisis), SPY (Crisis), BRENT (Calm)
   - *w18 Equivalent*: Sunken Gloom

7. **Terminal Twitch (Hyper-Alert Exhaustion)**: High alarm, low vitality, low aggression. Features: High facial tension but low textural "life," weary alert.
   - *Members*: ^VIX (Calm), GC=F (Calm)
   - *w18 Equivalent*: Hyper-Alert

8. **Vibrant Jitters (Manic Anxiety)**: High vitality, high distress, low aggression. Features: Erratic movement (implied), flushed, "busy" or restless features.
   - *Members*: RB=F (Calm), BRENT (Crisis), DX=F (Calm/Crisis), XLE (Crisis), ^TNX (Calm)
   - *w18 Equivalent*: New (Expanded Dynamic Range)

## Cross-Asset Differentiation
- **Energies (BRENT, WTI, RB, HO)**: These show the highest dynamic range. The move from WTI's "The Void" (Calm) to "Wolf of Wall Street" (Crisis) suggests a total regime shift in market character.
- **Safe Havens (^TNX, GC=F)**: Characterized by a "heavy" response. GC=F moves into "Sunken Gloom" while ^TNX finds "Gilded Zen" (likely reflecting yield compression).
- **Equities (SPY, XLE)**: SPY's descent from the "Bristling Director" (authority) to "Sunken Gloom" (defeat) is a classic ICU triage marker for market health.
- **Volatility (^VIX)**: Moves from "Terminal Twitch" to "The Void," perfectly capturing the transition from anticipation to total realization of crisis.

## Intermediate Expression Assessment
The reduction of calm-day alarm has been highly effective. By providing a "floor" of genuine neutrality (Corporate Beige) and composure (Gilded Zen), the subsequent spikes into panic or aggression feel earned. We no longer have the "perpetual panic" noise that plagued earlier iterations.

## Dynamic Range Analysis
**WIDER**. W19 achieves ≥3 bits of information density by populating the previously empty "Neutral" and "Composed" zones of the expression space. The delta between the calmest Calm (NG=F) and the most extreme Crisis (RB=F) is visually and quantitatively larger than in w18.

## Recommendations for w20
1. **Aggression Scaling**: DX=F is trapped in a jittery baseline. We need to decouple its aggression signal from its volatility signal to allow for more regime-based movement.
2. **Vitality Floors**: Encourage "The Void" to go even colder for extreme tail-risk events.
3. **Maturity Interplay**: Ensure that the high maturity of safe havens (like ^TNX) doesn't mask their vitality shifts.
