# Clinical Evaluation Report: w3-blind Synthetic Renders

## Methodology
Evaluation of 16 synthetic face renders (A-P) was performed blind to the underlying parameters. Each render was assessed for Arousal (1-10), Valence (-5 to +5), and clinical presentation.

## Summary Analysis

### 1. How many distinct emotional clusters?
The system demonstrates **5 distinct emotional clusters**:
- **Manic/Elated:** High arousal, high positive valence (A, H, N).
- **Panicked/Terrified:** High arousal, high negative valence (F, J, L).
- **Content/Calm:** Low-to-moderate arousal, positive valence (D, I, M).
- **Depressed/Apathetic:** Low arousal, negative valence (B, G, K, O).
- **Neutral:** Very low arousal, near-zero valence (E, P).

### 2. Can the system render LOW-AROUSAL NEGATIVE states?
**Yes.** Subjects B, G, K, and O clearly represent low-arousal negative states. The system effectively modulates the intensity of these states, ranging from mild apathy (K) to deep melancholic depression (G, O).

### 3. Can the system render HIGH-AROUSAL POSITIVE states that feel distinct from HIGH-AROUSAL NEGATIVE?
**Yes.** There is a sharp distinction between the high-arousal positive (Manic) cluster and the high-arousal negative (Panicked) cluster. 
- **Manic states** (A, H, N) utilize broad zygomaticus major activation (wide smiles) paired with wide eyes, conveying exuberance.
- **Panicked states** (F, J, L) utilize jaw drops, tense ocular muscles, and "gasping" mouth shapes, conveying acute distress.
The two states are unlikely to be confused in a clinical setting.
