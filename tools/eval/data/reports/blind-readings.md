## Meta-Analysis

### Emotional Clusters
I identified 6 distinct emotional clusters across the 24 faces:
1.  **High-Arousal Negative (The "Threat" Group):** Faces K (Rage), M (Horror), T (Fear). These are reactive, survival-oriented states.
2.  **Low-Arousal Negative (The "Distress" Group):** Faces D (Grief), V (Sadness), I (Disappointment). These are reflective, internalizing states.
3.  **High-Arousal Positive (The "Thrill" Group):** Faces X (Ecstasy), R (Thrill), J (Eagerness). These are expansive, reward-oriented states.
4.  **Low-Arousal Positive (The "Peace" Group):** Faces L (Contentment), H (Joy), C (Smugness/Pride). These are stable, ego-strengthening states.
5.  **Dominance/Aggression (The "Power" Group):** Faces A (Irritation), W (Intimidation), Q (Contempt). These are social-positioning states.
6.  **Baseline/Engagement (The "Social" Group):** Faces F (Neutral), S (Attentive), G (Puzzled), U (Playful).

### Ambiguity and Contradictions
- **The Hardest to Read:** Face G was the most ambiguous. The asymmetry in the brows combined with a neutral mouth makes it hard to distinguish between "skepticism," "confusion," or even a "minor neurological tic."
- **Contradictory Stories:** Face P (Overwhelmed) was interesting—the eyes say "Fear" (wide aperture) but the brow and mouth say "Effort/Anger" (furrowed, tense). This suggests a state of "Performance Pressure" where the person is afraid of failing but pushing through with aggression. Contempt (Face Q) also feels contradictory, as it combines a "happy" mouth corner with "angry" eyes.

### Top Half vs. Bottom Half Information Loss
- **Top Half Only:** If I could only see the top half, I would lose the nuances of social signaling (the smirk of O, the snarl of A, the triumph of X). However, I would still have a 90% accurate read on the **arousal level** and **basic valence** (Sad vs. Happy) due to the brow position and eye aperture.
- **Bottom Half Only:** If I could only see the bottom half, I would lose the **micro-expression nuances** that distinguish fear from surprise, or irritation from intimidation. The "smile" of a fearful person (Face T) can look surprisingly like the "smile" of a happy person (Face H) if the eyes are covered. The bottom half provides the "social mask," while the top half provides the "emotional truth."
