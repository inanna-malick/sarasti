## 0. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The objective of this evaluation is to determine which facial expression system—Variant C (Naturalistic Tension) or Variant D (Jaw-Decoupled Tension)—provides the highest "signal-to-noise" ratio for a trader monitoring a 25-seat floor during a systemic liquidity event. 

In our world, speed of recognition isn't a luxury; it's the difference between catching a bid and being the bid. When a desk is bleeding out, I don't have time to perform micro-expression analysis on a "naturalistic" brow furrow. I need to know, at a glance, who is still fighting the tape (High Tension) and who has surrendered to the margin call (Low Tension).

**Verdict:** Variant D is the only viable candidate. Variant C, while aesthetically "correct," suffers from catastrophic signal drowning at negative valence levels beyond -0.6. Variant D’s radical move of the jaw opening from the Valence axis to the Tension axis creates a binary visual "switch" that survives even the most extreme "Black Swan" scenarios.

## 1. COMPARATIVE ANALYSIS BY VALENCE LEVEL

### 1.1 Level: v = -0.3 (The "Choppy Session")

At this level, we’re looking at standard market friction. A -3% move in tech, perhaps a surprise CPI print. It’s annoying, but not existential.

*   **Variant C (vc_diag_calm_v-0.3 vs vc_diag_tense_v-0.3):**
    *   The calm face shows a slight, weary frown. The mouth is just barely parted—a "mouth breather" look that suggests mild irritation.
    *   The tense face sharpens significantly. The eyes narrow into a "predatory" focus. The skin texture around the nasolabial folds tightens.
    *   **Readability:** High. You can see the trader shifting from "watching" to "working an order." The head pose shift in C adds a nice layer of "leaning into the screen."

*   **Variant D (vd_diag_calm_v-0.3 vs vd_diag_tense_v-0.3):**
    *   The calm face is remarkably "stony." With the jaw closed, the v=-0.3 frown looks controlled. It’s the face of a professional who has seen this before.
    *   The tense face pops open the jaw. It’s an immediate shift in urgency.
    *   **Readability:** Extreme. The open jaw in D acts like a "Tension LED." If that mouth is open, something is happening. If it's closed, the trader is under control.

### 1.2 Level: v = -0.6 (The "Correction / Stop-Loss Cascade")

Now we’re hitting the puke points. Stop-losses are being triggered. The order book is thinning out.

*   **Variant C (vc_diag_calm_v-0.6 vs vc_diag_tense_v-0.6):**
    *   Both faces have open mouths now, because in Variant C, the jaw follows Valence. The "calm" face looks like it's mid-sigh—a look of "Here we go again."
    *   The "tense" face adds the brow/eye geometry. The trader looks angry. 
    *   **The Problem:** The signal starts to blur. The mouth is open in both. The primary difference is the "intensity" of the upper face. On a small monitor or from across the room, you might miss the brow furrow. All you see are two guys with their mouths open. Is he panicking? Or just tired of the sell-off? It’s ambiguous.

*   **Variant D (vd_diag_calm_v-0.6 vs vd_diag_tense_v-0.6):**
    *   **Calm:** The jaw is CLOSED. This is huge. The face looks like "Cold Anger." This is a trader who is taking the loss but remains analytical. He’s "brooding."
    *   **Tense:** The jaw is OPEN. This is "Hot Anger." This trader is shouting at a broker or frantically adjusting a hedge.
    *   **Readability:** Perfect. The "Given Up vs Fighting" distinction starts to emerge here. The closed-mouth v=-0.6 (Calm) is a "Calculated Loss." The open-mouth v=-0.6 (Tense) is a "Desperate Hedge."

### 1.3 Level: v = -0.9 (The "Systemic Collapse / Lehman Moment")

Total liquidation. The bid is gone. We are looking at the abyss.

*   **Variant C (vc_diag_calm_v-0.9 vs vc_diag_tense_v-0.9):**
    *   **Calm:** The mouth is a gaping maw of "Angry Valence." 
    *   **Tense:** The mouth is the *same* gaping maw. The only difference is a slightly more aggressive brow and some skin-tightening.
    *   **The Failure:** At v=-0.9, the Tension signal is effectively **DROWNED**. If I have 25 of these faces on my monitor, they all look the same: Screaming Skulls. I can't tell who is still trying to save the firm and who has already cleared out their desk. The facial geometry has reached its "Saturation Point."

*   **Variant D (vd_diag_calm_v-0.9 vs vd_diag_tense_v-0.9):**
    *   **Calm:** Jaw is CLOSED. Eyes are wide, brows are high/low in a look of total, frozen shock. This is the face of a man who just saw his net worth evaporate in 40 milliseconds and is paralyzed. It is the "GIVEN UP" face.
    *   **Tense:** Jaw is OPEN. It’s a primal scream. This is the "PANICKING" face. 
    *   **Readability:** Stark. Violent. The closed mouth at v=-0.9 is actually *scarier* than the open mouth. It indicates a total cessation of activity. In a crisis, that’s the guy you need to check on first—he might have stopped hedging entirely.

## 2. THE DROWNING TEST (v = -0.9)

This is the most critical metric for a crisis monitor. In a crash, everyone has "Bad Valence." Valence becomes a constant. The only variable that matters is Tension (Urgency/Arousal).

In **Variant C**, Tension is a "modifier" on top of a dominant Valence signal. When Valence is -0.9, the "Angry Mouth" is so large and visually noisy that it consumes the bandwidth of the viewer. You literally cannot see the brow furrow because the jaw is pulling all the facial muscles into its orbit. C fails the Drowning Test. It becomes a wall of undifferentiated rage.

In **Variant D**, Tension is the "primary operator" for the most significant facial feature: the jaw. By moving the jaw to the Tension axis, we ensure that the most visible change on the face is reserved for the most important information (Are they active or paralyzed?). D passes the Drowning Test with flying colors. Even at 10% opacity or a 100x100 pixel resolution, I can see if a mouth is open or closed.

## 3. THE TRADER'S INTUITION: "PANICKING" VS "GIVEN UP"

Imagine I’m the Head of the Desk. It’s 2:15 PM on a Tuesday. The S&P is down 7% and the circuit breakers are about to trip. I look at my monitor.

**Using Variant C:**
I see 25 faces that look like they're being electrocuted. Everyone has their mouth open. Everyone looks miserable. I have no idea who to talk to. I have to spend 5 seconds looking at each face to see if their eyes are "tense" or "calm." By the time I've checked 5 guys, the market has moved another 100 points.

**Using Variant D:**
I see a sea of "Screaming Skulls" (Open Mouths) and "Frozen Statues" (Closed Mouths).
- **The Screaming Skulls (Tense):** They are Panicking. They are still clicking. They are still fighting. I leave them alone; they are busy trying to survive.
- **The Frozen Statues (Calm):** They have Given Up. They are staring at their screens with closed mouths and dead eyes. They have reached "Terminal Valence" but have zero Tension. These are the guys who have stopped entering orders. These are the guys who are going to blow up the firm because they're too shocked to hedge.

Variant D lets me triage my desk in 2 seconds. I go straight to the "Frozen Statues" and tell them to get their heads back in the game.

*   **Trader Action:** "Look at the desk. They're not screaming anymore. They're just... gone. This is the bottom. Start looking for long entries."

## 5. VERDICT: WHY D IS THE ONLY CHOICE

Variant C is for animators who want "realistic" faces. Variant D is for engineers who want "functional" faces.

In a financial crisis, "Naturalism" is the enemy of "Legibility." We are building a *monitor*, not a movie. The decoupling of the jaw in Variant D is a stroke of genius for this specific use case. It takes the most powerful visual signal on the human face (the mouth) and assigns it to the most critical variable (urgency).

**Final Recommendation:**
Implement **Variant D** immediately. The loss of "naturalistic" mouth-frowning at v=-0.9 is a small price to pay for the ability to distinguish between a trader who is actively managing risk and one who has suffered a total psychological breakdown.

One minor tweak: In Variant D, ensure the "Closed Mouth" at v=-0.9 doesn't look *too* neutral. It needs to look like a "Grimace" or a "Locked Jaw" rather than a "Relaxed Mouth." The current diagnostic images (vd_diag_calm_v-0.9.png) capture this well—the tight lips suggest a suppression of emotion or a frozen state, which is exactly what we need.

**WE ARE GOING WITH D.**
